# Volume Price Analysis — Worked Examples Across Markets and Time Frames

This section consolidates VPA principles through real chart examples spanning US equities, ETFs, spot forex, index futures, commodity futures, tick charts, and weekly long-term charts. The recurring message: volume reveals what price alone conceals — effort versus result, insider accumulation and distribution, and the patience required to act on, or wait for, true confirmation.

## Honeywell (HON) — Sell-off, Absorption and Bullish Breakout

**What it is:** A daily stock chart sequence showing a complete VPA cycle: distribution, stopping action, absorption in a congestion zone, and breakout to new highs.

**Price action:** Initial weakness marked by a small shooting star, confirmed by a wide spread down candle. Then narrow spread down candles appear, followed by a hammer, gapped-up opens, sideways congestion, and finally wide spread up candles on the breakout.

**Volume:** Rising on the initial sell-off, then narrow spreads appear on rising volume (anomaly). High volume on the hammer. Average volume on the gapped-up open, then falling volume during the sideways drift. High volume returns on the breakout.

**Reading (effort vs result):** A narrow spread down candle with higher volume than the preceding bar is the classic stopping-volume anomaly — effort is present, but the result (downside progress) is absent. Later in the congestion phase, narrow spread down candles on high volume signal that selling pressure is being absorbed, not driving price lower.

**Insiders / psychology:** The smart money is soaking up supply at the lows and during the congestion, marking price down to build positions. The gapped-up open on average volume is a tentative test, not a commitment. Only when wide spread up candles arrive on rising volume do the insiders confirm the move.

**Signal:** The combination of stopping volume, hammer, and absorbed selling warns of a base forming. A breakout on rising volume with wide spreads validates the reversal. Falling volume during a sideways drift is not bearish — it shows the sellers have dried up at that level.

**Takeaway:** A bearish market requires falling prices AND rising volume. Falling prices on falling volume means the down move is exhausted, even if price has not yet turned.

## Duke Energy (DUK) — Patience, the Oil Tanker, and the Low Volume Test

**What it is:** A daily stock chart demonstrating that the initial VPA signal is rarely the final entry — accumulation and testing take time.

**Price action:** Early weakness after a wide spread up candle on only marginally higher volume. A price waterfall with falling prices, two attempts to rally (one with a wick to the upper body, one as a bullish engulfing), then two small hammers, then a low volume test, then a gap up day, congestion, another gap up breakout, a slow decline on low volume, a bullish engulfing candle, and a final gapped-up move into prior resistance.

**Volume:** Initial bull move on relatively low volume (anomaly). Rising volume on the waterfall, then average volume on the rally attempts. Low volume on the pullback hammers. A low volume test bar. Strong volume on the gap up days. Falling volume on the slow drift lower (anomaly). Low volume on the subsequent gap up into resistance (anomaly).

**Reading (effort vs result):** Rising price on nearly equal volume to a bar of half the spread is an early warning of weakness — the effort is insufficient for the result. A bullish engulfing candle on average volume, after a waterfall, is not yet a sign of strength; the test is whether follow-through arrives. The slow drift lower on falling volume is the dual of the sell-off anomaly: no selling effort, so no further downside.

**Insiders / psychology:** Smart money was distributing into the early rise, then absorbing the waterfall. The two small hammers on low volume are the final phase of mopping up supply. The low volume test is the insiders testing the market for residual selling — when it passes, they are ready to mark the price up. A gapped-up move on low volume into known resistance is a potential trap up move; the insiders may be testing supply before distributing again.

**Signal:** The low volume test after stopping volume is the highest-confidence bullish trigger. A gap up on low volume into prior resistance is a warning — be doubly on guard. Falling volume on a drift lower suggests the down move is a pause, not a reversal.

**Takeaway:** Expect to be wrong on the first signal. The oil tanker takes time to stop. The low volume test is the insiders telling you the ground is prepared.

## SLV (5-Minute Chart) — Scalping in Context, Hammer and Aftermath

**What it is:** An intraday ETF chart used to illustrate VPA within a scalping framework, and the importance of context (the dominant trend).

**Price action:** Sideways drift, then five consecutive down candles breaking support, a sideways drift, two narrow spread down candles, a hammer, a weak up candle, a wide spread up candle, a low volume test, a higher move on solid volume, a wide spread up candle and failure at the same level, then a drift.

**Volume:** Rising volume on the breakdown. First narrow down candle on above-average volume (anomaly), second on extremely high volume (stopping volume). High volume on the hammer. Low volume on the first up candle (weakness). Rising volume with the wide spread up candle (encouragement). Low volume on the test.

**Reading (effort vs result):** A narrow spread down candle on very high volume that should have driven price sharply lower is stopping volume — the buying absorbed the selling. The first up candle after a hammer on low volume is a weak response; the second candle (wide spread up on high volume) is the real confirmation.

**Insiders / psychology:** The market makers sold into weakness but are now absorbing supply and preparing to mark up. The low volume test confirms the test for supply has succeeded. The wide spread up candle that fails at the same level signals buyers are losing conviction.

**Signal:** A hammer alone is insufficient when the next candle cannot follow through. A scalp trader who entered on the hammer had to tolerate a weak response and judge the next bar before deciding to hold, scale in, or exit.

**Takeaway:** A hammer is a signal to watch, not necessarily a signal to act. The follow-through candle is what determines whether the insiders are joining the move. Multiple time frame analysis would have warned the scalp trader that the broader trend was bearish, making the trade high-risk by definition.

## GLD (15-Minute Chart) — Selling Climax, Failed Rallies, and Panic

**What it is:** An intraday gold ETF chart in a confirmed bearish environment, demonstrating how the insiders prop and dump the market through a selling climax.

**Price action:** Gap-down open on extremely high volume. Small hammer. Small candle with upper wick. Two down candles with lower wicks. Penultimate candle suggests stopping volume. Last down candle closes on average volume. First up candle — narrow spread, deep upper wick. Second up candle — narrow spread, average volume. Third up candle — narrow spread, but ultra high volume. Fourth up candle — even worse, ultra high volume and no progress. Narrow spread doji on high volume. Sell-off on ultra high volume. Narrow spread down candle with deep wick on very high volume. Repeat on next candle. Attempted rise that fails back to the open. Small hammer on ultra high volume. Three bullish candles, all narrow spread. Reversal lower on increasing volume. Very narrow doji on high volume. Wide spread up candle on above-average volume. Two more up candles on falling volume. Sideways drift on low volume. Weak close.

**Volume:** The dominant signature is ultra high volume accompanying candles that go nowhere. Narrow spreads on ultra high volume; rising volume as price falls; falling volume as price rises.

**Reading (effort vs result):** This is the textbook selling climax: effort (volume) is enormous, but the result (downside progress) is being nullified by the insiders propping the market. The market is rising on flat or falling volume, and falling on rising volume. When the prop is removed, the market lurches lower.

**Insiders / psychology:** The market makers are selling stock accumulated in the waterfall, using the narrow spread up candles on ultra high volume to offload inventory before driving price lower. The doji on high volume is a final pause before the next leg down. Later, as buyers emerge at the lows, the insiders absorb them on the narrow spread down candles with deep wicks. The three bullish candles on flat volume show the market is rising on no real demand, primed for another reversal.

**Signal:** Ultra high volume with a narrow spread is a loud signal of weakness. If it were buying, the market would be rising fast. The follow-through day opens gapped down on three times the previous day's open volume — panic selling — and even a hammer cannot slow the momentum.

**Takeaway:** Effort without result is the signature of insider activity. When volume is extreme but price does not move, the smart money is either propping or distributing. The next session's open will reveal which.

## GLD — Follow-On Session (Panic Selling and Capitulation)

**What it is:** The session following the GLD selling climax, showing how insider distribution is confirmed by panic volume.

**Price action:** Gapped-down open. Three candles with extreme volume. A hammer. A solitary wide spread up candle on high volume that fails to follow through. Congestion. Four down candles on rising volume.

**Volume:** Three candles exceeding 6 million each against an average of around 500,000 — more than ten times average. Panic.

**Reading (effort vs result):** The panic volume on the open is confirmed selling. The hammer, despite its volume, cannot stem the momentum. The single up candle is unconfirmed, and the four down candles that follow with rising volume signal further heavy selling ahead.

**Insiders / psychology:** The market makers who propped the previous session have finished distributing; now the late longs and stops are flushed. Volume of this magnitude is rarely a turning point in itself; the question is whether stopping volume appears at lower levels.

**Signal:** Extreme volume at the open, against the prior day's bearish backdrop, is capitulation. Trade with the trend until true stopping volume appears at a lower level.

**Takeaway:** When volume is many multiples of average, read the price action carefully — it is often the climactic end of a move, not the start of a new one.

## AUD/USD 15-Minute — Shooting Stars, Falling Volume Pullbacks, and Position Holding

**What it is:** A spot forex intraday trade showing entry, management, and exit using only VPA.

**Price action:** Steady rise on average volume. A blue candle with a wide body and equally deep upper wick. Two bars of higher prices. First shooting star with high volume. Narrow spread doji with high volume. Second shooting star with higher volume and a lower high. Sell-off, with pullbacks, then a series of attempts to rally that fail, before stopping volume and a congestion phase.

**Volume:** Average throughout the rise. The first warning candle shows a large volume bar (selling) despite a higher close. Rising volume on the shooting stars. Above-average volume on the first pullback after the sell-off. Falling volume on each subsequent attempt to rally. Dropping volume as the congestion phase develops.

**Reading (effort vs result):** The deep upper wick on a wide-bodied candle with a large volume bar is the first warning — selling is present in size, but the candle still closed higher. Two shooting stars in succession with rising volume confirm the reversal. Pullbacks in a downtrend on above-average volume followed by attempts to rise on falling volume confirm the trend remains intact. A pullback that has not been preceded by stopping volume is just a pause.

**Insiders / psychology:** The smart money is distributing into the rise. The first warning bar shows them selling into strength. The shooting stars mark exhaustion. Once the trend is established, the market makers allow the pullbacks on modest volume to exhaust themselves before resuming the move lower. Stopping volume at the bottom signals the insiders are absorbing supply and preparing for the next phase.

**Signal:** A second shooting star at a lower high with higher volume is a short entry trigger. Falling volume on counter-trend rallies confirms the trend. Stopping volume signals the end of the leg.

**Takeaway:** Once in a position, continually re-apply VPA to manage it. Falling volume on pullbacks tells you the trend is intact; rising volume on pullbacks would warn of stopping action. Position management is VPA in real time.

## AUD/USD Weekly — Selling Climax

**What it is:** An 18-month weekly chart showing a complete cycle: bullish congestion, exhaustion, selling climax, and reversal higher.

**Price action:** Bullish phase. Congestion on average volume. Narrow spread up candle. Narrow spread candle on ultra high volume. Hammer with deep wick. Small hammer as a low volume test. Steady march higher over several months. Two wide spread candles in a yellow box. Selling climax. Break lower. Weak rally attempts. Further breakdown.

**Volume:** Average during congestion. Very high on the first anomaly candle. Ultra high on the second. Low on the test. A slow, steady fall in volume over the months-long rise. Two volume bars in the yellow box that have fallen to almost nothing. Ultra high volume on the selling climax. Narrow spread up candles on very high volume during the weak rally attempts (further weakness signal).

**Reading (effort vs result):** The two narrow spread candles on ultra high volume at the top of the trend are the climactic exhaustion: effort is enormous, but the result (upside progress) is absent. The market makers have withdrawn. The slow steady fall in volume over a multi-month rise is the first warning — demand is drying up. When the selling climax arrives, the volume is enormous on narrow spreads, then price breaks lower.

**Insiders / psychology:** During the long rise, market makers were slowly distributing. As the trend matured, late buyers (fear of missing out) piled in just as the insiders were leaving. The narrow spread up candles on very high volume during the weak rally after the break are the insiders marking price up one last time to offload remaining inventory before taking it lower.

**Signal:** Wide spread candles on collapsing volume at the top of a long trend is a major warning. A narrow spread candle on ultra high volume is the climactic exhaustion bar. Subsequent narrow spread up candles on very high volume during a weak rally confirm further downside.

**Takeaway:** Be flexible with volume in long trends. The principle is preserved — the trend must have above-average or rising volume to continue — but a steady decline in volume is the early warning that demand is fading, even when no individual bar is dramatically out of line.

## AUD/USD Weekly — Buying Climax and the Resistance Ceiling

**What it is:** An 18-month weekly chart showing a price waterfall, brief attempt to rally, buying climax, and eventual break higher through resistance.

**Price action:** Topping out into a price waterfall. A hammer. A small shooting star. Entry into a buying climax phase. Narrow spread up candle with deep upper wick. Two more candles, one of them a wide spread on ultra low volume. Roll-over back into the congestion zone. Ceiling of resistance marked by two yellow lines. Trend higher with pauses over nine months. Selling climax at the end.

**Volume:** Rising selling volumes validate the waterfall. The hammer is followed by a small shooting star on high volume — not enough stopping volume. Narrow spread up candle on high volume during the climax — not ready to rise. Very low volume on the next two candles — the second is wide spread on ultra low volume. A breakout through resistance would require above-average, steady, rising volume (not necessarily explosive). A gap-up breakout would require volumes well above average, or ultra high if the move is dramatic.

**Reading (effort vs result):** A wide spread candle on ultra low volume is the buyers' exhaustion at the climax. A narrow spread up candle with a deep upper wick on high volume shows the market is being marked up but not progressing — the insiders are propping. A breakout on steady, above-average volume is the genuine sign; a gap up on weak volume is a trap.

**Insiders / psychology:** The market makers let the waterfall run, then prop the market at the lows to offload the shorts they accumulated higher. The wide spread candle on ultra low volume shows buyers are exhausted; the insiders then drive the market back into the prior range. Eventually, they are ready to mark up, and the breakout through resistance on steady volume signals a real change of character.

**Signal:** Buying climax characteristics: narrow spread up candles on high volume, a deep upper wick, then wide spread on ultra low volume. The opposite of the selling climax template.

**Takeaway:** Resistance ceilings require volume validation to break. Normal breakouts need above-average, rising volume; gap-up breakouts need well above average, sometimes ultra high, to be genuine.

## YM E-mini (5-Minute) — The Open, the Gap, and the Wave Structure

**What it is:** An intraday index futures chart showing the open of the session, a gap up, and a steady trend higher on wave structure.

**Price action:** Gapped-up open. Wide spread up candle. Two down candles (profit taking on the gap). Steady rise with minor pullbacks. Congestion on the right with declining volume.

**Volume:** High volume on the open candle. Falling volume on the two down candles. The trend higher has waves of buying volume, with each subsequent wave slightly lower than the previous — interest waning. The congestion phase has falling volume.

**Reading (effort vs result):** The two down candles after a gap up on falling volume are not a reversal — they are profit taking. The buyers remain in control. As the trend progresses, the buying volume is slightly lower on each successive wave, suggesting the move is running out of steam, but no anomaly has yet appeared. The fall in volume into the congestion confirms interest is drying up.

**Insiders / psychology:** The big operators joined the gap-up open. The early profit taking was the weak longs being shaken out before the trend continued. The slow decline in volume into the congestion phase signals that even the operators are winding down.

**Signal:** A gapped-up open on high volume with a wide spread up candle is the big operators joining. A subsequent pullback on falling volume is a buying opportunity, not a reversal. Declining wave volumes over an extended trend warn of exhaustion.

**Takeaway:** In trends, allow latitude in volume interpretation. Each wave does not need to be higher than the last; what matters is that the overall effort supports the move and that no major anomaly appears.

## ES E-mini (10-Minute) — The Open Trap

**What it is:** Three consecutive sessions on the S&P 500 E-mini, focused on the open, the previous night's electronic trade, and a classic trap-up move.

**Price action:** End of session one: shooting star on an extremely high volume bar (a "telegraph pole"), then an up candle that goes nowhere, then a small doji. Day two opens at the same level, with a wide spread up candle on low to average volume (the trap). Day three opens gapped up, on high volume well above day two's open.

**Volume:** The closing bar of day one is an extreme outlier — a telegraph pole. The up candle that follows is on very high volume but produces no price progress. The day two open is on low to average volume despite a similar price spread to the prior up candle (a substantially lower volume for a similar move = weakness). Day three opens on volume well above the previous day's open.

**Reading (effort vs result):** The shooting star on extreme volume at the session close is the big operators clearing positions before the next day. The up candle on very high volume that goes nowhere is selling into a propped market. The wide spread up candle on the next day's open with much lower volume is the trap — the price spread is the same as the previous up candle, but the volume is substantially lower. The day three gap up on volume well above day two's open is the genuine move.

**Insiders / psychology:** The big operators prepared the trap the night before, distributing into the propped market. Day two's open lures eager traders into long positions, frightened of missing the move. The selling then resumes. The day three open, with proper volume, is the operators buying into the genuine trend.

**Signal:** A wide spread up candle on low or average volume after a session-end distribution is a classic trap up move. The volume comparison to the prior up candle with the same spread is the diagnostic. Gapped-up opens on high volume relative to the prior session are genuine.

**Takeaway:** Always compare volume to the price spread, not just to recent bars. A similar spread on much lower volume is weakness. Trap up moves happen at the open in every market and every time frame.

## Coffee Futures (80-Tick Chart) — Tick Volume as Activity, and Distortion

**What it is:** An intraday commodity futures chart on a tick-based chart, showing how tick speed reinforces volume analysis, and how extreme volume can distort the view of what follows.

**Price action:** Weak open higher. Roll over and slide lower. Volume spikes and the market marches north in wide spread up candles. Consolidation with narrow spread up candles and upper wicks. Roll over and sell off. Attempted recovery on falling volume. Shooting star. Price waterfall. Recovery from the waterfall with little buying or stopping volume evident.

**Volume:** Falling on the initial slide. Spikes higher as the operators arrive. Ultra high on the 9th bar, but the price reaction is weaker than the prior bars (anomaly). Above average on the consolidation. High on the sell-off. Falling on the recovery attempt. Distorted by the prior extreme volumes — the bottom of the waterfall had well-above-average volume, but appeared muted by comparison.

**Reading (effort vs result):** The 9th bar's ultra high volume with a weaker reaction than the surrounding wide spread candles is the first sign of weakness — the operators are struggling. The consolidation candles with upper wicks confirm it. The recovery attempt on falling volume confirms the trend is still down. The waterfall follows. The lack of obvious buying or stopping volume at the bottom is suspicious, but partly explained by the prior extreme volumes distorting the visual scale.

**Insiders / psychology:** The large operators moved in and pushed price up until they began to struggle, as evidenced by the ultra high volume bar with a weak reaction. They then consolidated to offload inventory before driving price down. The shooting star triggered the waterfall. The next session saw further selling.

**Signal:** Ultra high volume with a price reaction that does not match the surrounding bars is weakness. Recovery attempts on falling volume confirm the downtrend. When volume is extreme, calibrate your reading of subsequent bars against the distorted baseline.

**Takeaway:** Tick charts give a second view of activity through candle-formation speed, complementing the volume bars. Be aware that extreme volumes can distort your judgment of subsequent bars — know what constitutes average volume for the instrument.

## DJIA Weekly — Long-Term Accumulation and the Patient Investor

**What it is:** A multi-year weekly chart of the Dow Jones Industrial Average showing major accumulation phases and a long bullish trend.

**Price action:** Major rounded tops, multiple accumulation bases, and a multi-year rise. A yellow box marks an 11-week period of strong operator buying, followed by six to eight weeks of further accumulation at the same level. Total consolidation of 4–5 months. Subsequent rise on average volume.

**Volume:** Concentrations of volume in the accumulation zones. Average volume on the subsequent rise. No extremes either way. No selling climax visible.

**Reading (effort vs result):** The accumulation volume is concentrated at the base. The subsequent rise on average volume is healthy — effort is consistent with the result. The absence of any selling climax on the weekly chart means the major trend is not at risk.

**Insiders / psychology:** The market makers spent 4–5 months accumulating positions. The rounded tops that followed are characteristic distribution phases by other participants. The market is in the hands of patient insiders who will not move until they are fully positioned.

**Signal:** For long-term investors, the weekly chart is the timeframe. The lack of any selling climax is a sign the trend is intact. A selling climax on the weekly would be the warning of a major reversal.

**Takeaway:** VPA works identically on every timeframe. The longer the timeframe, the larger the operator footprint, and the more patient you must be. The market makers cannot hide volume; they can only try to disguise individual orders, but the aggregate activity is visible to anyone who looks.
