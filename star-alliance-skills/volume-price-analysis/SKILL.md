# Volume Price Analysis — the Merchant's craft

Anna Coulling's synthesis of Wyckoff, Wall Street's tape-reading tradition, and the modern chart. Price tells you *what* happened; volume tells you *whether it was real*. The whole craft is one question asked of every bar: does the volume (the **effort**) confirm the price move (the **result**), or contradict it? When they agree, the move is genuine; when they diverge, the smart money — the "insiders," the market makers — are doing the opposite of what the price pretends. This craft is read-only: the Merchant reads the story volume tells, names the phase of the insider cycle, and hands the read across the table. The pen, never the purse.

## What it is / is not

- IS: reading a chart through the price-volume relationship — candle spread/wick anatomy, the effort-vs-result test and its anomalies, the accumulation/distribution/testing/climax cycle, VPA candle signals, support/resistance, trends and pivots, and Volume at Price. Identification and interpretation, with Coulling's market psychology.
- Is NOT japanese-candlesticks: that craft reads the candle *line and pattern* (Nison). This craft reads the candle *together with its volume* (Coulling) — they are complementary layers of the technical read; use both for confluence.
- Is NOT market-recon: market-recon is the wide read of a market (fundamentals, structure, positioning, catalysts). This craft is the volume-and-price layer of the technical read feeding into that report.
- Is NOT trading-strategy: this craft reads the chart; trading-strategy turns a read into a mechanical entry/exit/sizing plan. A VPA signal is evidence, not an order.
- Is NOT execution: never places trades, never moves money, never writes broker code. The output is a read on parchment.

## Core principles (read these first)

1. **Volume must confirm price.** A genuine move is backed by appropriate volume — rising price on rising volume, falling price on rising volume. The single most important sentence in the craft.
2. **Effort vs result.** Volume is effort; the price spread is the result. When a large effort produces a small result (high volume, narrow spread) or a small effort a large result (low volume, wide spread), that *anomaly* is the signal — the insiders are absorbing or faking.
3. **The insiders cannot hide volume.** Market makers can disguise individual orders but not the aggregate. Volume is the one public footprint of accumulation and distribution.
4. **Read the market cycle.** Every move is a phase: accumulation (insiders buy at wholesale), markup, distribution (insiders sell at retail), markdown — punctuated by testing, selling climax, and buying climax. Name the phase before naming the trade.
5. **Three-step read, multiple timeframes.** Close up on the latest candle, zoom to its neighbors, zoom to the whole chart — and confirm across timeframes. The structure is identical on a 5-minute and a daily chart; only the duration differs.
6. **Read-only boundary.** Read, name the phase, grade. Never size a live book, never order, never transfer.

## How you work

1. **Frame the chart.** Establish the trend, the timeframe, and where price sits relative to congestion/support/resistance.
2. **Test every bar: effort vs result.** For each significant candle, compare volume (effort) to spread (result). Flag confirmations and, more importantly, anomalies.
3. **Name the candle signal.** Identify VPA signals (hammer, shooting star, doji, stopping volume, topping-out volume) *with their volume*, pulling the exact reading rules from the matching reference file.
4. **Locate the phase.** Place the action in the insider cycle — accumulation, distribution, a test, or a climax — and say what the insiders are doing.
5. **Map with VAP.** Where relevant, read Volume at Price for the horizontal high-volume nodes that mark true support/resistance. Order is always VPA first, VAP second.
6. **Grade and hand off.** Deliver a dated, plain read with a Low/Med/High confidence and a clear "what would change this view." Fuse with `japanese-candlesticks` for confluence. Never act on it.

## Sharpening the craft

The apprentice reads the candle; the journeyman reads the candle and its volume together; the master reads where that bar sits in the insider cycle and waits for volume to confirm the breakout. A wide green candle on thin volume is not strength — it is a trap; a narrow candle on enormous volume at the top of a run is not a pause — it is distribution. The chart never lies, but the price alone will deceive you.

- Volume confirms price, or the move is a lie. Start every read there.
- Hunt the anomaly — effort without result, or result without effort, is where the signal lives.
- Name the phase of the cycle before you name the trade.
- Fuse with `japanese-candlesticks` for pattern confluence; VPA before VAP, always.
- Stay read-only. The craft ends at the read; sizing and orders belong to other hands.
