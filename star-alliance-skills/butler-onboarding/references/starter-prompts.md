# Starter prompts

The deliverable of onboarding is **2-3 concrete starter prompts** the Guild Master can
pick from — not a guess about what they meant, and not a barrelling-ahead first action.
A starter prompt is a one-line, ready-to-run task phrased the way *they* would say it,
each one a different honest direction the request could take.

## Why a menu, not a guess

A vague request fans out into several legitimate intents. Picking one and executing is a
coin-flip — and if it's wrong, the Guild Master pays for the rework. A menu makes the
choice cheap and explicit: they read three lines and tap one. The Butler trades one round
of "which of these?" for not building the wrong thing.

## What makes a good starter prompt

1. **Runnable, not abstract.** "Audit the transactions page for slow queries" — not
   "improve performance." The Guild Master should be able to send it back verbatim.
2. **Spans the real fork.** The 2-3 prompts should cover *genuinely different* readings of
   the request, so the pick is informative. Three near-duplicates waste the choice.
3. **Carries its owner.** Each prompt implies the member it routes to, so picking one is
   also picking a formation. You don't have to name the member, but you should know it.
4. **Honestly sized.** If one option is a campaign and another is a one-liner, say so —
   "(a quick pass)" vs "(a bigger, multi-step build I'd have the Strategist plan)".

## The shape to deliver

> Here are a few ways I'd start — pick one, or tell me none of these fit:
>
> 1. **[Outcome A]** — [one runnable line]. *(quick pass · the Developer)*
> 2. **[Outcome B]** — [one runnable line]. *(a build · the Architect, then the Developer)*
> 3. **[Outcome C]** — [one runnable line]. *(ongoing · the Herald)*

Keep the owner annotation light or drop it for non-technical Guild Masters — it's there so
*you* can dispatch instantly once they pick.

## Worked example

> **Guild Master:** "Help me with my business." → (after one discovery answer: "more
> customers")

> Three honest starting points for "more customers" — pick one:
>
> 1. **Fix the positioning first** — have the Herald audit how Lex Council presents itself
>    and sharpen the message before spending on outreach. *(quick, high-leverage)*
> 2. **Build a lead engine** — the Herald plans a content + SEO + email sequence to bring
>    inbound leads over the next quarter. *(a campaign — I'd have the Strategist plan the
>    waves)*
> 3. **Mine the relationships you have** — sweep existing contacts and conversations for
>    warm leads worth re-opening. *(fast, uses what you already have)*

Each is a different real direction, each runnable, each routes cleanly. The Guild Master
picks; the Butler dispatches.

## After the pick

Once they choose, hand off to `members-formation` — the chosen prompt is now a *clear*
task with an owner, which is exactly that skill's input. Onboarding's job ends the moment
the fog lifts.
