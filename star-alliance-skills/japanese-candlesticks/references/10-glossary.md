# Glossary & Visual Dictionary

## Abandoned Baby
- **Type:** major reversal (top or bottom) · 3 candlestick lines.
- **Structure:** a doji star that gaps away (including shadows) from both the prior candlestick and the following candlestick. The middle session is a doji whose entire range (high–low, including shadows) sits isolated above (top) or below (bottom) the surrounding candles.
- **Recognition:** locate a doji in the middle of three sessions; confirm an upward gap (top) or downward gap (bottom) on both sides of the doji such that neither the prior nor the following candle's range overlaps the doji's range.
- **Psychology:** the prior trend exhausts itself, complete indecision (a doji) appears in isolation, and the gap on the other side of the doji signals a full transfer of control to the opposite side.
- **Signal & confirmation:** a major top or bottom reversal; equivalent to a Western island top/bottom in which the island session is itself a doji.
- **Nison's notes:** described as very rare.

## Advance Block
- **Type:** bearish reversal variation · 3 candlestick lines.
- **Structure:** a variation of three white soldiers in which the last two white real bodies show weakening upside progress — either via tall upper shadows or via progressively smaller real bodies.
- **Recognition:** three white candlesticks with rising closes, but the second and/or third show notably long upper shadows or shrinking bodies versus the first.
- **Psychology:** buying force is diminishing or selling pressure is increasing even though prices are still printing higher white candles.
- **Signal & confirmation:** signifies a diminution of buying force or an increase in selling pressure — a top warning.
- **Nison's notes:** a weakening variant of three white soldiers.

## Belt-hold Line
- **Type:** bullish or bearish reversal · 1 candlestick line.
- **Structure:** 
  - Bullish belt hold: a tall white candlestick that opens on its low (also called a white opening shaven bottom).
  - Bearish belt hold: a long black candlestick that opens on its high (also called a black opening shaven head).
- **Recognition:** a single long-bodied candle at a price extreme with the open at the extreme of the range and a long body extending in the opposite direction.
- **Psychology:** the open at the extreme followed by a one-way move to the opposite end of the range shows one side taking immediate control from the open.
- **Signal & confirmation:** bullish at a low price area; bearish at a high price level.
- **Nison's notes:** synonymous with white opening shaven bottom (bullish) and black opening shaven head (bearish).

## Candlestick Lines and Charts
- **Type:** chart construction.
- **Structure:** traditional Japanese charts whose individual lines look like candles. Each candlestick line is comprised of a real body and shadows.
- **Recognition:** each session is drawn as a real body (open-to-close) with thin lines (shadows) extending above and below to the session's high and low.
- **Psychology:** the candle visually captures the session's directional outcome (body) and the rejected extremes (shadows).
- **Signal & confirmation:** the foundational building block for every candlestick pattern in the book.
- **Nison's notes:** the name comes from the candle-like appearance of the lines.

## Counterattack Lines
- **Type:** bullish or bearish reversal (stalemate) · 2 candlestick lines.
- **Structure:** following a black (white) candlestick in a downtrend (uptrend), the market gaps sharply lower (higher) on the open and then closes unchanged from the prior session's close.
- **Recognition:** a directional gap on the open that is fully erased by the close, with the second session's close equaling the first session's close.
- **Psychology:** the gap shows initial aggression by one side, but the close back at parity shows the other side has absorbed the move — a stalemate between bulls and bears.
- **Signal & confirmation:** signals indecision/stalemate rather than immediate continuation.
- **Nison's notes:** the close is unchanged from the prior session's close.

## Dark-Cloud Cover
- **Type:** bearish reversal · 2 candlestick lines.
- **Structure:** in an uptrend, a long white candlestick is followed by a black candlestick that opens above the prior white candlestick's high and then closes well into the white candlestick's real body.
- **Recognition:** a long white candle, then a black candle with a gap-up open and a close that penetrates substantially into the prior white body.
- **Psychology:** bulls opened with strength (gap up), but bears overwhelmed them by session's end, pushing the close deep into the prior white body.
- **Signal & confirmation:** bearish reversal signal.
- **Nison's notes:** the second candle must open above the prior high and close well into the white real body.

## Dead Cross
- **Type:** bearish signal · moving-average crossover.
- **Structure:** a short-term moving average crosses under a longer-term moving average.
- **Recognition:** the shorter MA line drops below the longer MA line.
- **Psychology:** shorter-term momentum turning down through the longer-term average suggests a shift to bearish control.
- **Signal & confirmation:** bearish signal.
- **Nison's notes:** a Western moving-average concept used in combination with candlesticks.

## Doji
- **Type:** reversal · 1 candlestick line.
- **Structure:** a session in which the open and close are the same (or almost the same). Different varieties (gravestone, long-legged, etc.)
