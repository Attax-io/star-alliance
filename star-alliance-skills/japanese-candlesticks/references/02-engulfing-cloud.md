# Engulfing, Dark-Cloud Cover, Piercing & Neck Lines

## Engulfing Pattern (General)

- **Type:** Reversal pattern (used as both bullish bottom reversal and bearish top reversal) · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Two candlesticks. The second real body must engulf (envelope) the prior real body; it need not engulf the shadows.
- **Recognition:**
  - The second real body of the engulfing pattern should be the opposite color of the first real body.
  - Exception to the opposite-color rule: if the first real body is so small that it is almost a doji (or is a doji), then it can be the same color as the second. Thus, after an extended downtrend, a tiny white real body engulfed by a very large white real body could be a bottom reversal. In an uptrend, a minute black real body enveloped by a very large black real body could be a bearish reversal pattern.
- **Psychology:** The two-line geometry depicts a decisive transfer of control from one side to the other on the second session.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Acts as a major reversal indicator. Confirmation characteristics that increase the importance of the signal:
  1. The first day has a very small real body and the second day has a very long real body. This reflects a dissipation of the prior trend's force followed by an increase in force behind the new move.
  2. The pattern appears after a protracted or very fast move. A protracted trend increases the chance that potential buyers are already long, leaving less of a supply of new longs to keep the market moving up. A fast move makes the market overextended and vulnerable to profit taking.
  3. Heavy volume on the second real body. This could be a blow off.
  4. The second day engulfs more than one real body.
- **Nison's notes:** As with all charting techniques, there should be some latitude allowed; perfection is not required. The candlestick engulfing pattern is the closest Japanese analog to the Western reversal day but may give reversal signals not available with the Western reversal day, allowing the analyst to get a jump on bar-chart reversal signals.

## Bullish Engulfing Pattern

- **Type:** Bullish · bottom reversal · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** A black real body followed by a white real body. The second (white) real body engulfs the prior (black) real body.
- **Recognition:** Identified by a white real body that engulfs the prior black real body, occurring in a downtrend (or sometimes at the bottom of a lateral band). The pattern must appear after a downtrend (or at the bottom of a lateral band) to warrant action.
- **Psychology:** The bears drive prices down on the first session, but the bulls reverse the move and engulf the prior session's body on the second session, signaling a shift in control.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Bottom reversal. The pattern's strength is enhanced by the general factors for engulfing patterns (small first body/large second body, occurrence after a protracted or fast move, heavy second-day volume, engulfing of more than one prior body).
- **Nison's notes:** A bullish engulfing pattern that occurs outside of a downtrend does not warrant action. If a long, white real body closes above the highs of the pattern, it could presage another rally. If the market closes under the lows of the bullish engulfing pattern by way of a long black candlestick, then another downleg should resume.

## Bearish Engulfing Pattern

- **Type:** Bearish · top reversal · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** A white real body followed by a black real body. The second (black) real body engulfs the prior (white) real body.
- **Recognition:** Identified by a black real body that engulfs the prior white real body, occurring after an uptrend.
- **Psychology:** The bulls drive prices up on the first session, but the bears engulf the prior session's body on the second session, indicating a transfer of control.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Top reversal. Strength is enhanced by the general engulfing factors. The engulfing pattern may give a reversal signal that is not available using the criteria for a Western reversal day, since a Western top reversal day (or month) requires that a new high be made for the move, which a bearish engulfing pattern's black real body need not have done.
- **Nison's notes:** A bearish engulfing pattern is a more meaningful top reversal than a dark-cloud cover, just as a total solar eclipse (engulfing) is more meaningful than a partial eclipse (dark-cloud cover). If a long, white real body closes above the highs of the bearish engulfing pattern, it could presage another rally. There is always room for subjectivity in chart reading.

## Dark-Cloud Cover

- **Type:** Bearish · top reversal · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Two candlesticks. The first day is a strong white real body. The second day's price opens above the prior session's high (above the top of the upper shadow). However, by the end of the second day's session, the market closes near the low of the day and well within the prior day's white body. The dark-cloud cover's black real body only gets partially into the white body (a partial solar eclipse), whereas a bearish engulfing pattern's black real body covers the entire previous white real body (a total solar eclipse).
- **Recognition:**
  - First day: strong white real body.
  - Second day: opens above the prior session's high.
  - Second day closes well within the prior day's white real body.
  - The greater the degree of penetration of the black real body's close into the prior white real body, the greater the chance for a top.
  - Some Japanese technicians require more than a 50% penetration of the black session's close into the white real body. If the black candlestick does not close below the halfway point of the white candlestick, it may be best to wait for more bearish confirmation following the dark-cloud cover.
- **Psychology:** The market is in an uptrend. A strong white candlestick is followed by a gap higher on the next session's opening; the bulls are in complete control. But no continuation of the rally occurs. The market closes at or near the lows of the day, moving well within the prior day's real body. The longs have second thoughts about their position. Those waiting to sell short now have a benchmark to place a stop — at the new high of the second day of the dark-cloud cover.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Top reversal (after an uptrend or, at times, at the top of a congestion band). Factors that intensify the importance of dark-cloud covers:
  1. The greater the degree of penetration of the black real body's close into the prior white real body, the greater the chance for a top. If the black real body covers the prior day's entire white body, a bearish engulfing pattern occurs (the more meaningful top reversal).
  2. During a prolonged uptrend, if a strong white day opens on its low (shaven bottom) and closes on its high (shaven head), and the next day reveals a long black real body opening on its high and closing on its low, then a shaven head and shaven bottom black day has occurred.
  3. If the second body (the black body) of the dark-cloud cover opens above a major resistance level and then fails, it proves the bulls were unable to take control of the market.
  4. If, on the opening of the second day, there is very heavy volume, a buying blow off could have occurred: many new buyers jump aboard, then the market sells off. Old and new longs will eventually realize the move has failed. For futures traders, very high opening interest can be another warning.
- **Nison's notes:** If a long, white real body closes above the highs of the dark-cloud cover, it could presage another rally. Variations on the ideal pattern are acceptable: a dark-cloud cover in which the second day's black real body opened at the prior day's high instead of above it is still a warning sign and a negative factor. The concept of multiple technical indicators corroborating one another (e.g., a dark-cloud cover coinciding with a failed test of a prior support-now-resistance level, or a failure at a resistance zone) is important. Some flexibility is allowed in the 50%-penetration rule for dark-cloud covers, more so than for the piercing pattern.

## Piercing Pattern

- **Type:** Bullish · bottom reversal · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Two candlesticks in a falling market. The first candlestick is a black real body day. The second is a long, white real body day. This white day opens sharply lower, under the low of the prior black day. Then prices push higher, creating a relatively long, white real body that closes above the mid-point of the prior day's black real body. The piercing pattern's white real body only pierces the prior black body; in the bullish engulfing pattern, the white real body engulfs the previous black real body.
- **Recognition:**
  - First day: black real body.
  - Second day: opens sharply lower, under the prior black day's low.
  - Second day closes above the mid-point of the prior day's black real body.
  - An ideal piercing pattern has a white real body that pushes more than halfway into the prior session's black real body.
  - The greater the degree of penetration into the black real body, the more likely a bottom reversal.
- **Psychology:** The market is in a downtrend. The bearish black real body reinforces this view. The next day the market opens lower via a gap; the bears watch with contentment. Then the market surges toward the close, closing sharply above the prior day's close. The bears will be second-guessing their position. New lows could not hold, and those looking to buy see that it may be time to step in from the long side.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Bottom reversal. The piercing pattern signal increases in importance based on the same factors (1) through (4) as the dark-cloud cover, but in reverse. The piercing pattern's strength can be enhanced when it coincides with a successful test of a prior low (suggesting a double bottom).
- **Nison's notes:** There is less flexibility with the piercing pattern than with the dark-cloud cover. The piercing pattern's white candlestick should push more than halfway into the black candlestick's real body, because three other patterns (on-neck, in-neck, and thrusting) share the same basic formation but are viewed as bearish when the white real body gets less than halfway into the black's real body. A variation is acceptable: a piercing pattern in which the white real body opened under the prior day's real body, but not under the prior day's low, is still a warning sign that the prior downleg was running out of steam, provided the white real body closed more than 50% into the prior day's black real body. If the market closes under the lows of the piercing pattern by way of a long black candlestick, then another downleg should resume. The piercing pattern may forecast the end of selling pressure even when an immediate rally does not follow — the market may simply stabilize for an extended period before rallying.

## On-Neck Pattern

- **Type:** Bearish · continuation in a declining market · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Same basic form as the piercing pattern. A black real body day is followed by a white real body. The on-neck pattern's white candlestick (usually a small one) closes near the low of the previous session.
- **Recognition:** Identified by a small white candlestick closing near the low of the prior black session, in a downtrend.
- **Psychology:** The market is in a downtrend. The black real body reinforces the bearish trend. The next day's white candle fails to push meaningfully into the prior black body, closing only near its low. The trend remains intact.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Bearish. With this pattern (and the in-neck and thrusting patterns), as prices move under the white candlestick's low, the trader knows that it is time to sell.
- **Nison's notes:** One of three patterns (on-neck, in-neck, thrusting) that share the same general formation as the piercing pattern but are bearish because the white real body fails to push more than halfway into the black's real body. It is not important to remember the individual names of these three patterns; just remember the principle that the white candlestick should push more than halfway into the black candlestick's real body to send a bottom reversal signal.

## In-Neck Pattern

- **Type:** Bearish · continuation in a declining market · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Same basic form as the piercing pattern. A black real body day is followed by a white real body. The in-neck pattern's white candlestick closes slightly into the prior real body, and should also be a small white candlestick.
- **Recognition:** Identified by a small white candlestick that closes slightly into the prior black real body, in a downtrend.
- **Psychology:** The market is in a downtrend, and the white candlestick's failure to push meaningfully into the prior black body confirms that the bears remain in control.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Bearish. With this pattern (and the on-neck and thrusting patterns), as prices move under the white candlestick's low, the trader knows that it is time to sell.
- **Nison's notes:** One of three patterns (on-neck, in-neck, thrusting) sharing the same general formation as the piercing pattern but bearish because the white real body fails to push more than halfway into the black's real body. Memorizing the individual names is not necessary; the underlying principle — that the white candlestick should push more than halfway into the black candlestick's real body to send a bottom reversal signal — is what matters.

## Thrusting Pattern

- **Type:** Bearish in a declining market; bullish in a rising market · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Same basic form as the piercing pattern. A black real body day is followed by a white candlestick. The thrusting pattern is a longer white candlestick, stronger than the in-neck pattern, but still does not close above the middle of the prior black real body.
- **Recognition:** Identified by a longer white candlestick that closes below the mid-point of the prior black real body (bearish in a declining market), or by two thrusting patterns occurring within several days of each other (bullish).
- **Psychology:** In a declining market, the white candlestick shows some buying pressure but not enough to penetrate the midpoint of the prior black body, leaving the bears in control. In a rising market, the same geometry reflects continued buying pressure. Two thrusting patterns in close succession reinforce a bullish bias.
- **Signal & confirmation:**
  - Bearish in a declining market: as prices move under the white candlestick's low, the trader knows it is time to sell.
  - Bullish if it occurs in a rising market.
  - Bullish if it occurs twice within several days of each other.
- **Nison's notes:** One of three patterns (on-neck, in-neck, thrusting) that share the same general formation as the piercing pattern. Individual names do not need to be memorized; the key is the principle that the white candlestick must push more than halfway into the black candlestick's real body to be a bottom reversal signal.

## Western Reversal Day (Comparison Concept)

- **Type:** Western technical-analysis concept used as a comparison to the engulfing pattern.
- **Structure:** A Western reversal day occurs when, during an uptrend (or downtrend), a new high (or low) is made with prices closing under (or above) the prior day's close.
- **Recognition:** A new extreme (high for an uptrend, low for a downtrend) combined with a close opposite to the prior session's close.
- **Psychology:** Reflects an intraday attempt by the prevailing trend to extend, followed by a reversal that closes into the prior session's range.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Western reversal signal, but requires a new extreme (new high for a top, new low for a bottom) before it can be recognized.
- **Nison's notes:** The Japanese candlestick engulfing pattern is the closest Japanese analog to the Western reversal day. However, the engulfing pattern may give reversal signals not available with the Western reversal day, allowing the analyst to get a jump on those who use traditional reversal days. Because the Western top reversal day requires a new high for the move, candlestick bearish engulfing patterns whose black real body periods do not make new highs are recognized by Japanese candlesticks as reversals but would not be recognized in the United States using Western criteria.

## Shaven Head and Shaven Bottom Black Day

- **Type:** Bearish · top-reversal intensification factor · two-candle pattern.
- **Structure:** Occurs during a prolonged uptrend. The first day is a strong white day that opens on its low (shaven bottom) and closes on its high (shaven head). The next day is a long black real body that opens on its high and closes on its low.
- **Recognition:** A strong, gap-less white day (open at low, close at high) followed by a long black day (open at high, close at low) at the end of a prolonged uptrend.
- **Psychology:** The bulls drive the market to new highs with maximum force on the first day, but the bears respond with equal force on the second day, opening at the high and closing at the low — a complete reversal of the prior session's structure.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Reinforces the importance of a dark-cloud cover pattern as a top-reversal factor. By itself, the pattern's identification is part of the dark-cloud cover's intensification criteria.
- **Nison's notes:** Listed as the second factor increasing the importance of a dark-cloud cover. A strong, gap-less white day followed by a long black day that opens at the high and closes at the low creates the shaven head and shaven bottom black day, which strengthens the bearish signal of a dark-cloud cover at the end of a prolonged uptrend.

## Support and Resistance Interchangeability

- **Type:** Western technical-analysis concept used in conjunction with candlestick patterns.
- **Structure:** A prior support level, once broken, can convert to new resistance.
- **Recognition:** A price level that acted as support on a prior occasion is observed to act as resistance on a later attempt to rally through it.
- **Psychology:** Traders who missed selling at the prior support level recognize their error when the level is breached, and are willing to sell when the market retests the level from the other side.
- **Signal & confirmation:** A failed rally attempt that retests a broken support level from above and is rejected confirms the resistance role of that level. Dark-cloud covers coinciding with such resistance tests are especially meaningful.
- **Nison's notes:** The concept of support/resistance interchangeability is examined in detail in Chapter 11 of the book. The corollary principle is that multiple technical indicators corroborating one another (e.g., a dark-cloud cover coinciding with a failed test of a former support-now-resistance level) is the focus of the second half of the book, which covers the combination of candlestick techniques with other technical tools.
