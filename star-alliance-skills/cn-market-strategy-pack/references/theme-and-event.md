# Theme, Event & Fundamental Strategies

Strategies driven by catalysts, narratives, and fundamentals rather than pure price
structure: tracking hot themes, trading discrete events, repricing expectations, and
grading growth quality. The common discipline — separate *substantive* benefit from
mere concept-association, and gauge how much is already priced in. All **read-only**.

---

## hot_theme — Hot Theme (热点题材)

- **Thesis:** When policy, industry, technology, or herd capital ignites a theme, the
  stocks with *substantive* exposure outperform; the edge is telling real
  beneficiaries from concept-chasers. Tied to rules 2, 3, 5, 7.
- **Selection logic:** Gauge theme strength via sector rankings — is the related
  sector leading on gain, turnover, and attention, and is the move *spreading* from a
  few core names to many? A single-stock spike with no sector resonance is weak.
  Gauge the stock's relevance from news (business, orders, capacity, customers,
  filings): classify as substantive / indirect / weakly-associated beneficiary; flag
  repricing risk when a weakly-associated name has run too far. Confirm relative
  strength — gain, volume ratio, and turnover stronger than the sector average;
  strong theme stocks show volume, active turnover, and pullbacks that hold key MAs.
- **Entry:** Do not chase at accelerating, high-deviation levels. Triggers: a pullback
  that gets absorbed, a volume breakout, or continued sector resonance.
- **Exit:** Stop when the theme rolls from spread into divergence/ebb, or on a major
  negative — regulatory inquiry or clarification filing can veto the rating outright.
- **Regime fit:** sector_hot. Map the theme phase: launch / spread / divergence / ebb.

## event_driven — Event Driven (事件驱动)

- **Thesis:** Discrete corporate or industry events — earnings pre-announcements,
  order wins, M&A/restructuring, policy landing, product launches, penalties,
  litigation — create catalysts; the job is to judge catalyst strength, realisation
  probability, and risk boundary. Tied to rules 3 and 5.
- **Selection logic:** Classify the event (earnings / policy / order-product /
  capital-action / regulatory-risk) and pin its date — stale or undated information
  cannot be the primary basis. Trace the impact path: does it move revenue, margin,
  valuation, financing capacity, market share, or only sentiment? For big orders or
  policy wins, state the realisation horizon and uncertainty; for regulatory,
  reduction, penalty, or litigation events, risk takes priority. Check market reaction
  via quote and trend — has price already fully reflected the event? A volume rally
  that has not cleared key resistance can wait for confirmation; a high-level
  volume stall, or a spike-and-fade after good news, warns of realisation pressure.
- **Entry:** Before realisation, emphasise position control and a defined time window;
  after realisation, re-grade — switch from "expectation trade" to "earnings
  verification."
- **Exit:** Every recommendation must carry an invalidation: announcement misses
  expectations, a key support breaks, or the event's heat fades.
- **Regime fit:** sector_hot, volatile.

## expectation_repricing — Expectation Repricing (预期重估)

- **Thesis:** Markets reprice as expectations of earnings, policy, industry cycle,
  valuation centre, or competitive position shift; the edge is reading whether current
  price reflects *expectation repair*, *expectation failure*, or *expectation
  overheating*. Tied to rules 3, 5, 6.
- **Selection logic:** Identify what recently changed expectations (pre-announcements,
  institutional views, orders, policy, product progress, industry data) and separate
  hard information (filings, financials, orders) from soft (rumour, opinion, mood).
  Read the expectation-gap direction: positive gap = market was pessimistic, new
  information shows business/earnings better than expected; negative gap = market was
  optimistic, new information undershoots or verification fails; if a continuous rally
  has already priced it, flag realisation risk. Test valuation repricing against
  fundamentals — a higher PE/PB must be matched by earnings quality, growth
  durability, and industry room; on the way down, distinguish a one-off disturbance
  from a long-logic change. Confirm with trend whether the repricing has become a
  trend (volume breakout = capital-confirmed; shrinking-volume bounce = repair watch).
- **Entry:** Positive gap not yet fully priced is the constructive setup; specify the
  observation point (next earnings, order realisation, policy landing).
- **Exit:** Positive gap already realised by a big rally, or a negated core
  assumption, downgrades the view; a high-level volume stall or good-news-no-rally
  signals weakening expectation.
- **Regime fit:** volatile, sector_hot.

## growth_quality — Growth Quality (成长质量)

- **Thesis:** Combining revenue/profit growth, ROE, cash flow, and industry room
  identifies high-quality compounders and flags growth that is stalling — a
  medium-to-long-term lens, not a short-term chart read. Tied to rules 2, 3, 5.
- **Selection logic:** **Growth** — read revenue, net profit, operating cash flow, and
  ROE; check that revenue and profit growth move together (watch for "revenue up,
  profit not"); if there is only concept-heat with no financial verification, lower the
  growth certainty. **Quality** — higher and stable ROE is better; operating cash flow
  agreeing in direction with net profit signals reliable earnings quality, while cash
  flow much weaker than profit flags receivables/inventory/collection risk.
  **Valuation tolerance** — use PE/PB and market cap to judge whether the market has
  pre-spent the growth; high growth tolerates higher valuation only if growth covers
  it; high valuation + slowing growth should sharply cut the score. **Trend
  confirmation** — when fundamentals are good but the chart has not confirmed, give an
  observation condition rather than chasing.
- **Entry:** Growth-appropriate buy points — a breakout after earnings verification, a
  pullback to a long-term MA, or a valuation reset.
- **Exit/risk:** Classify the company as high-quality growth / growth-verifying /
  growth-slowing / growth-falsified, and downgrade on "revenue up but not profit" or
  deteriorating cash flow.
- **Regime fit:** trending_up.
