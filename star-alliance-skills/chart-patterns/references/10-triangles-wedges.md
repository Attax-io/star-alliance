# Triangles & Wedges

## Triangles, Ascending

**Type:** Continuation; bullish reversal; appears in uptrend (downward breakouts are bearish continuations).

**Identification:** Triangle with a horizontal top trend line (resistance) and an up-sloping bottom trend line (support). At least two minor highs touch the horizontal top, and at least two minor lows touch the up-sloping bottom. Prices should cross the pattern several times (no large white space in center). Volume is heavier at the start, tapers off, and is usually low just before the breakout. Breakout is a close outside either trend line; prone to premature breakouts whose volume is indistinguishable from the genuine breakout.

**Psychology:** A supply of shares is available at a fixed price (e.g., a fund selling into a ceiling). Buyers emerge at successively higher lows until the supply is exhausted; without the overhang, demand pushes prices up out of the top.

**Measure rule:** Compute the formation height (highest high minus lowest low at the start of the formation). For upward breakouts, add the height to the horizontal top trend line value; for downward breakouts, subtract the height from the breakout price. Alternative: draw a line parallel to the up-sloping trend line from the top-left corner; its value at breakout day is the target.

**Key numbers:** Volume trend downward. Upward breakouts: bull market average rise 35%, break-even failure 13%, throwbacks 57%, meet target 75%; bear market average rise 30%, failure 12%, throwbacks 54%, meet target 63%. Downward breakouts: bull market average decline 19%, failure 11%, pullbacks 49%, meet target 68%; bear market average decline 24%, failure 3% (lowest of any pattern for small moves), pullbacks 45%, meet target 66%. Change after trend ends: roughly –29% to –32% after upward breakouts, +47% to +52% after downward breakouts. Busted-pattern performance: 41% (bull up), 20% (bear up), –22% (bull down), –28% (bear down). Throwbacks/pullbacks hurt performance.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for breakout confirmation (close outside trend line). Use the measure rule for the price target; be ready to take profits near it. Sell on a downward breakout; if short, cover immediately on an upward breakout (about 43% break downward). Place stop below the formation low. For best performance: trade with the market trend (long in bull, short in bear); trade busted patterns; avoid triangles in the middle of the yearly range; select tall patterns; use heavy breakout volume for upward breakouts and light breakout volume for downward breakouts; avoid overhead resistance; expect the breakout about 62% of the way to the apex.

## Triangles, Descending

**Type:** Continuation; bullish continuation (upward breakouts) / bearish reversal (downward breakouts).

**Identification:** Triangle with a horizontal bottom trend line (support) and a down-sloping top trend line (resistance). At least two distinct minor lows touch the horizontal base, and at least two distinct minor highs touch the down-sloping top. Prices cross from side to side with no large white space. Volume typically recedes and often drops to a low just before the breakout. Breakouts occur on high or low volume. Two of three break out downward.

**Psychology:** Investors establish a floor by buying at a perceived fair value. Each rebound is smaller than the last (down-sloping tops). Eventually buying demand exhausts, the support gives way, and remaining holders sell into the breakdown.

**Measure rule:** Compute the height (highest high minus lowest low). For downward breakouts, subtract the height from the horizontal support line value; for upward breakouts, add the height to the breakout price. Alternative: draw a line parallel to the down-sloping trend line from the lower-left corner; its value at breakout is the target.

**Key numbers:** Volume trend downward. Upward breakouts: bull market average rise 47%, failure 7%, throwbacks 37%, meet target 84% (best combination); bear market rise 27%, failure 9%, throwbacks 52%, meet target 61%. Downward breakouts: bull market decline 16%, failure 16%, pullbacks 54%, meet target 54%; bear market decline 25%, failure 11%, pullbacks 59%, meet target 50%. Change after trend ends: roughly –30% to –34% after upward breakouts, +50% to +60% after downward breakouts. Busted-pattern performance: 52% (bull up). Days to ultimate high/low: 178/86/50/32. Throwbacks/pullbacks hurt performance.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for confirmation. Sell short on a downward breakout or after a pullback resumes down. Cover near the measure-rule target. For best performance: trade upward breakouts in a bull market or downward breakouts in a bear market; avoid throwbacks/pullbacks by checking for nearby overhead resistance or underlying support; expect the breakout 56% (bear) to 65% (bull) of the way to the apex; select tall patterns; tall and narrow (up breakouts) or tall and wide (down breakouts) work best; trade heavy breakout volume; be ready to exit quickly in bear markets.

## Triangles, Symmetrical

**Type:** Continuation; bullish continuation (upward) / bearish reversal (downward). Synonym: Coils.

**Identification:** Two trend lines — one down-sloping along the tops, one up-sloping along the bottoms — that converge at the apex. At least two distinct minor highs and two distinct minor lows; price should cross the pattern filling the white space. Volume usually recedes throughout the formation, often very low just before the breakout. Breakout direction is unknown ahead of time and follows the prevailing trend 54% of the time. Duration typically exceeds three weeks (shorter formations are pennants).

**Psychology:** Selling pressure checks each rally, value buyers catch each dip. Volume dries up, and once a large order pierces a trend line, buy-stop orders cascade, launching the breakout.

**Measure rule:** Compute the height (highest high minus lowest low). For upward breakouts, add the height to the highest high; for downward breakouts, subtract from the breakout price. Alternative: treat the triangle as a half-staff (midpoint of a move) and project accordingly.

**Key numbers:** Volume trend downward. Upward breakouts: bull market rise 31%, failure 9%, throwbacks 37%, meet target 66%; bear market rise 26%, failure 7%, throwbacks 55%, meet target 57%. Downward breakouts: bull market decline 17%, failure 13%, pullbacks 59%, meet target 48%; bear market decline 19%, failure 9%, pullbacks 62%, meet target 42%. Change after trend ends: roughly –31% to –33% after upward breakouts, +45% to +50% after downward breakouts. Busted-pattern performance beats originals: 40%/38%/-23%/-28%. Days to ultimate high/low: 124/77/45/30. Bear-market declines are steeper and shorter than bull-market rises.

**Trading tactics:** Trade with the prevailing trend. Always wait for the breakout. Trade busted patterns in the new direction. An intrapattern trade (buy at lower trend line, sell at upper, then short) is possible in tall/long formations. For best performance: trade with the market trend; trade busted patterns; select breakouts near the yearly low; avoid throwbacks/pullbacks; expect the breakout about 73% of the way to the apex; select tall patterns or, better, tall and narrow patterns; trade heavy breakout volume.

## Wedges, Falling

**Type:** Continuation; bullish reversal or continuation (upward) / bearish continuation (downward). Synonym: Descending wedges.

**Identification:** Two down-sloping, converging trend lines with the top line steeper than the bottom. At least five touches (three on one side, two on the other); avoid a horizontal bottom (then it is a descending triangle). Duration at least three weeks and rarely over four months. Volume usually trends downward (about 7 of 10 patterns).

**Psychology:** Half of wedges act as consolidations of the prevailing trend; the rest are reversals. Buyers and sellers reach an equilibrium that tightens like a coiled spring. The pent-up force releases on the breakout, sending prices sharply in one direction.

**Measure rule:** Upward breakouts: the highest high in the wedge is the price target. Downward breakouts: subtract the formation height (highest high minus lowest low) from the breakout price.

**Key numbers:** Volume trend downward. Upward breakouts: bull market rise 32%, failure 11%, throwbacks 56%, meet target 70%; bear market rise 26%, failure 11%, throwbacks 61%, meet target 60%. Downward breakouts: bull market decline 15%, failure 15%, pullbacks 69%, meet target 30%; bear market decline 24%, failure 6%, pullbacks 72%, meet target 36%. Change after trend ends: roughly –28% to –33% after upward breakouts, +51% to +53% after downward breakouts. Busted-pattern performance: 38% (bull up), 52% (bear up), –15% (bull down), –20% (bear down). Days to ultimate high/low: 116/77/43/32 — bear-market declines are steeper and shorter than bull-market rises. Reversals outperform continuations. About a quarter of wedges dip below the bottom trend line, circle, and head higher.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for a close outside the trend line. Buy after throwback or pullback completes and price resumes direction. Watch for the dip-and-reverse quirk in downward breakouts. For best performance: trade with the market trend; favor reversals; trade busted patterns; take profits quickly after downward breakouts in bear markets; expect the breakout just past halfway to the apex (most powerful bull-market upward breakouts occur 55% to 80% of the way to apex); select tall or wide patterns; use heavy breakout volume; gaps help performance in bull markets; avoid overhead resistance or underlying support.

## Wedges, Rising

**Type:** Continuation; bullish continuation (upward) / bearish reversal (downward). Synonym: Ascending wedges.

**Identification:** Two up-sloping, converging trend lines. At least five touches (three on one side, two on the other); a horizontal top makes it an ascending triangle, not a wedge. Duration at least three weeks, rarely more than three or four months. Volume usually trends downward and is low before the breakout. Forms most often near the end of an uptrend, occasionally as a retrace in a downtrend.

**Psychology:** Higher highs and higher lows tighten into a narrowing channel as buyers' enthusiasm wanes. Volume dries up. On a downward breakout, supply overwhelms demand and price collapses out of the bottom.

**Measure rule:** Downward breakouts: the lowest low in the formation is the price target. Upward breakouts: subtract the lowest low from the highest high and add the result to the breakout price.

**Key numbers:** Volume trend downward. Upward breakouts: bull market rise 28%, failure 8%, throwbacks 73%, meet target 58%; bear market rise 17%, failure 14%, throwbacks 66%, meet target 33%. Downward breakouts: bull market decline 14%, failure 24%, pullbacks 63%, meet target 46%; bear market decline 20%, failure 15%, pullbacks 63%, meet target 40%. Change after trend ends: roughly –30% to –35% after upward breakouts, +36% to +53% after downward breakouts. Busted-pattern performance beats originals: 36%/23%/-17%/-39%. Days to ultimate high/low: 127/60/38/38. Continuations outperform reversals after downward breakouts. Pullbacks hurt performance; gaps help when the breakout is in the market direction.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for breakout confirmation. Sell short after a downward breakout or buy after an upward breakout. If you miss the breakout, sell after a pullback or buy after a throwback once price resumes direction. Take profits quickly — postbreakout moves are meager. For best performance: continuations are best after a downward breakout; trade busted patterns when the new direction aligns with the market trend; select bull-market upward breakouts for lowest failure rates; avoid pullbacks; gaps help only when breakout aligns with the market trend; downward breakouts reach the ultimate low quickly (bear-market upward breakouts may top out after week two); select tall or wide patterns; use heavy breakout volume with upward breakouts.
