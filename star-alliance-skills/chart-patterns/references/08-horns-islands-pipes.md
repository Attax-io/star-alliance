# Horns, Island Reversals & Pipes

## Horn Bottoms

**Type:** Reversal; bullish; appears at the end of a downtrend or on a short retrace in an uptrend.

**Identification:** Two downward price spikes on a weekly chart, separated by a single week. The middle week's low stays well above the spike lows, giving an inverted-horn shape. Spikes should be longer than similar spikes over the prior year. The horn lows should sit clearly below surrounding prices (clear visibility, especially to the left). The pattern confirms when price closes above the highest high in the 3-week pattern.

**Psychology:** A sharp plunge (left spike) is followed by a one-week reversal-type week, then a retest of the low (right spike) that fails to make a new low. Selling pressure exhausts itself, the second spike closes near its high, and a base forms that reverses the prevailing downtrend.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low from the highest high in the horn, then add the difference to the highest high to obtain the target price.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: rank 14/23, break-even failure 9%, average rise 35%, throwbacks 29%, target met 76%, change after trend ends –32%, volume trend downward.
- Bear market: rank 6/19, break-even failure 7%, average rise 27%, throwbacks 58%, target met 61%, change after trend ends –37%, volume trend downward.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for confirmation (close above the pattern high). Buy immediately in uptrends, wait in downtrends to confirm the turn. Place a stop about $1 below the lowest horn low to absorb a retest. For best performance: select tall patterns with abnormally long spikes and heavy breakout volume; prefer horns with the right horn inside the trading range of the left, with a large price difference between the two lows; a lower left spike outperforms. Skip formations where nearby overhead resistance invites a throwback.

## Horn Tops

**Type:** Reversal; bearish; appears at the end of an uptrend or near the end of a long downtrend.

**Identification:** Two upward price spikes on a weekly chart, separated by a single week. The middle week's high sits well below the spike highs. Spikes should tower above surrounding prices and be larger than most other spikes over the prior year. The pattern confirms when price closes below the lowest low in the 3-week pattern.

**Psychology:** After a long advance, two tall peaks with heavy volume mark exhaustion of buying interest. A test of the low on the middle week fails to push prices to a new high, and once support breaks on the second gap, the downtrend resumes.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low from the highest high in the pattern, then subtract the difference from the lowest low to obtain the target price.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: rank 4/21, break-even failure 7%, average decline 21%, pullbacks 33%, target met 70%, change after trend ends 51%, volume trend downward.
- Bear market: rank 10/21, break-even failure 2%, average decline 22%, pullbacks 44%, target met 60%, change after trend ends 47%, volume trend downward.

**Trading tactics:** Trade only confirmed patterns (close below the pattern low). Avoid horns that form after a long downtrend where a trend change is already underway; they tend to fail or decline only briefly. For best performance: pick tall patterns, prefer the right horn as an outside week, look for light volume on both spikes in a bull market, and skip patterns with nearby underlying support that invites a pullback.

## Island Reversals

**Type:** Reversal; bullish bottoms (upward breakout) and bearish tops (downward breakout); appears after a prior trend move.

**Identification:** Two gaps bracket a price island at or near the same price level (within $0.13 in the study, each gap at least $0.25). Tops show prices rising into the left gap and falling away from the right; bottoms show the mirror image. Breakout volume is usually heavy. Island length can be one day (one-day reversal) to several months. Average length is just over a month.

**Psychology:** A breakaway gap establishes a new short-term trend, and an exhaustion gap at the same price level marks the end of that move. There must be a prior move to reverse; flat consolidation patterns fail. The island visually isolates the exhaustion from the surrounding trend.

**Measure rule:** Compute the formation height (highest high minus lowest low). For bottoms, add the height to the highest high; for tops, subtract the height from the lowest low.

**Key numbers:**
- Tops (downward breakout): Bull rank 21/21, break-even failure 17%, average decline 17%, pullbacks 65%, target met 62%, change after trend ends 45%, volume trend downward. Bear rank 13/21, break-even failure 5%, average decline 23%, pullbacks 59%, target met 46%, change after trend ends 46%, volume trend downward.
- Bottoms (upward breakout): Bull rank 23/23, break-even failure 18%, average rise 23%, throwbacks 70%, target met 69%, change after trend ends –28%, volume trend downward. Bear rank 11/19, break-even failure 10%, average rise 21%, throwbacks 75%, target met 49%, change after trend ends –37%, volume trend downward.

**Trading tactics:** Trade in the direction of the prevailing market trend for best results. Throwbacks and pullbacks are frequent and complete quickly (about a week); either wait for the return to the breakout price and a resumption of direction, or buy/sell immediately and exit when the gap fills. For best performance: choose patterns that are both tall and narrow; in a bull market favor wide patterns and in a bear market favor narrow ones; select breakouts near the yearly low except for downward breakouts in a bear market; watch for nearby support/resistance that invites a return to the gap; and wait for a trend-line close beyond the line before pulling the trigger.

## Long Islands

**Type:** Continuation (54% of the time) more often than reversal (46%); bullish on upward breakouts, bearish on downward breakouts; appears within a prevailing trend.

**Identification:** Two gaps that usually do not share the same price (18% of long islands also qualify as island reversals when gaps align). Each gap should be at least $1 wide to set the island apart from ordinary price action. Island length is less than 4 months. U-shaped volume is typical because volume spikes on each gap and recedes between them. Successive islands in a trend tend to get shorter; a sharply shorter island near the end of a run often flags a coming trend change.

**Psychology:** A gap creates a temporary trading range that floats free of the surrounding price action. The second gap, in the direction of the existing trend, confirms the island as a continuation pause. When multiple islands stack in a single trend, each shorter than the last, the final one often marks the trend's climax.

**Measure rule:** Compute the formation height from the highest high to the lowest low, divide by two, then add (upward breakout) or subtract (downward breakout) the result from the closing price the day before the second gap.

**Key numbers:**
- Upward breakout: Bull rank 15/23, break-even failure 11%, average rise 31%, throwbacks 67%, target met 82%, change after trend ends –35%, volume trend downward. Bear rank 5/19, break-even failure 4%, average rise 25%, throwbacks 74%, target met 72%, change after trend ends –37%, volume trend downward.
- Downward breakout: Bull rank 6/21, break-even failure 5%, average decline 22%, pullbacks 54%, target met 78%, change after trend ends 46%, volume trend downward. Bear rank 6/21, break-even failure 2%, average decline 26%, pullbacks 54%, target met 76%, change after trend ends 48%, volume trend downward.

**Trading tactics:** Trade with the prevailing market and industry trend. Be prepared for brief trades because a large share of islands reach the ultimate high or low within the first two weeks. For best performance: select tall patterns, prefer those narrower than the median, and watch for the unusually short island that often signals an approaching trend change; in a bull market, a falling volume trend outperforms; throwbacks and pullbacks hurt performance, so check for nearby support or resistance.

## Pipe Bottoms

**Type:** Reversal; bullish; appears at the end of a downtrend or as a retrace within an uptrend.

**Identification:** Two adjacent downward price spikes on a weekly chart, forming parallel twin lows. Spikes should be longer than the average downward spike over the prior year and the week before and after the pipe should have lows near the top of the pipe, so the formation stands alone. Large price overlap between the two weeks is required (do not pair a tall spike with a short one). Above-average volume on at least one spike is common. The pattern confirms when price closes above the highest high in the 2-week pattern.

**Psychology:** A sharp one-week decline is followed immediately by another similar decline, draining sellers. The second spike fails to make a new low and is followed by a third week of rising prices, leaving a V-shape and a clear support zone that the market uses as a launching pad.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low from the highest high in the pipe, then add the difference to the highest high to obtain the target price.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: rank 2/23, break-even failure 5%, average rise 45%, throwbacks 44%, target met 83%, change after trend ends –33%, volume trend downward.
- Bear market: rank 3/19, break-even failure 4%, average rise 32%, throwbacks 52%, target met 72%, change after trend ends –36%, volume trend downward.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for confirmation (close above the pattern high) before buying. Place a stop about half a point below the lowest pipe low to allow for a retest, and raise the stop as price rises. For best performance: trade pipes in a bull market; select tall patterns with a falling volume trend and heavy breakout volume; prefer heavy volume on the left spike and light volume on the right; choose a lower left spike and a right spike that is an inside week of the left; avoid nearby overhead resistance that invites a throwback.

## Pipe Tops

**Type:** Reversal; bearish; appears near the end of a long-term uptrend or as the top of an upward retrace within a downtrend.

**Identification:** Two adjacent upward price spikes on a weekly chart. The two spikes should be larger than most other spikes over the prior year, share a large price overlap, and have a small peak-to-peak price variation. The best-performing pipes appear at the top of a retrace in a prolonged downtrend. The pattern confirms only when price closes below the lowest low in the pipe.

**Psychology:** Twin peaks with strong volume mark exhaustion of buying interest during an advance. The second peak fails to make a new high, and once support gives way below the middle low, the prevailing decline resumes. In a sideways base, there is little prior rise to reverse, and the pipe often fails.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low from the highest high in the pipe, then subtract the difference from the lowest low to obtain the target price.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: rank 4/21, break-even failure 11%, average decline 20%, pullbacks 41%, target met 70%, change after trend ends 56%, volume trend downward.
- Bear market: rank 3/21, break-even failure 2%, average decline 27%, pullbacks 37%, target met 68%, change after trend ends 50%, volume trend downward.

**Trading tactics:** Trade only confirmed patterns. Short pipes in a bear market for the largest declines; avoid pipes that form at the end of a long downtrend where the trend may be reversing. Place a stop above the highest high to control loss if price recovers. For best performance: select tall pipes with above-average spike size; in a bear market, prefer spike volume lighter than the one-month average; look for a large price difference between the two highs and a lower left spike; avoid trades with underlying support that invites a pullback.
