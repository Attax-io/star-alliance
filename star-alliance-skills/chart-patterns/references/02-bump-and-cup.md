# Bump-and-Run Reversals & Cup with Handle

## Bump-and-Run Reversal Bottom

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal or continuation. Appears in a downtrend (handle = down-sloping lead-in).

**Identification:** Frying-pan shape with handle on the left. Use arithmetic scale. Down-sloping trend line along daily highs at roughly 30–45 degrees; higher on the left, sloping down (best performance). Lead-in height = widest vertical distance from trend line to daily low in the first quarter of the formation; lead-in must last at least one month. Bump phase: decline steepens to 60+ degrees on higher volume, then rounds over and turns up. Bump height (trend line to lowest low) should be at least 2× lead-in height. Uphill run carries price through the trend line. Volume is typically high at formation start, bump start, and upward breakout. Breakout = close above the down-sloping trend line. About 20% of patterns show a dual bump (second bump lower than first). Avoid short and narrow patterns.

**Psychology:** A study of momentum. Initial climb exhausts buying interest, smart money and momentum players exit, supply overwhelms demand, and the stock collapses as "problems with the company" emerge. Selling pressure eventually abates, the stock rounds over, and a basing/cup forms. As the stock pierces the trend line, institutional buying returns and an uphill run begins.

**Measure rule:** Target = the highest high in the pattern (top of the chart pattern). Prices reach this high 68% of the time in bull markets and 64% in bear markets.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 2% (bull) / 1% (bear). Average rise 38% (bull) / 31% (bear). Reversal performance 40% (bull) / 31% (bear); continuation 36% / 30%. Rises over 45% occur 34% of the time in bull, 25% in bear. Change after trend ends −29% (bull) / −34% (bear). Throwbacks occur 59% (bull) / 73% (bear) and hurt performance: with throwback 37%/28% rise vs. without 40%/39%. Heavy breakout volume helps bull (40%) but hurts bear (27%); light breakout volume 34%/35%. Performance with breakout gap 46% (bull) / 25% (bear). Busted pattern performance −28% (bull) / −24% (bear). Days to ultimate high 186 (bull) / 109 (bear). Dual bumps 20% (bull) / 21% (bear). Performance rank 8 of 23 (bull) / 3 of 19 (bear). Volume trend: upward. Best breakouts occur near the yearly low. Tall-and-wide patterns perform best; short-and-narrow worst.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for confirmation — buy when price closes above the down-sloping lead-in trend line. Place a stop 0.15 below the nearest support zone and raise the stop as price advances. Sell at or near the old high (start of the formation) when weakness shows, or sell half and trail the rest. Prefer breakouts near the yearly low, with a bump-to-lead-in height ratio near 2:1, and look for price weakness five to six weeks after the breakout; throwbacks hurt performance, so check for overhead resistance before entering.

## Bump-and-Run Reversal Top

**Type:** Short-term bearish reversal. Appears in an uptrend (rising trend line beneath the lows).

**Identification:** Mountain-range shape. Use arithmetic scale. Rising trend line along the lows at roughly 30–45 degrees; avoid flat or steep (over 60°) lines. Lead-in phase ≥ 1 month with a range of at least $1 from the highest high to the trend line in the first quarter. Bump phase: prices rise at 45–60°+ on high volume, round over, and decline back to the 30° trend line. Bump height (highest high to trend line) must be at least 2× lead-in height. Downhill run breaks below the trend line. Volume high at formation start, bump start, and at trend-line crossing. Breakout = close below the trend line. About 28% of patterns in bull markets (38% in bear) show a dual bump, with the second peak usually below the first.

**Psychology:** Initial uptrend reflects investor eagerness. A favorable event (earnings surprise, upgrade) pulls in momentum players, volume spikes, and a steeper new trend line forms. Supply catches up with demand, the smart money turns cautious, and the stock rounds over. Once the trend line is pierced, the smart money sells, panic follows, downward momentum builds, and only after the selling pressure abates do prices stabilize.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the lead-in height from the breakout price; the result is the minimum expected decline. About 8 of 10 stocks meet the target.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 5% (bull) / 1% (bear). Average decline 19% (bull) / 27% (bear). R/C performance: bull −20% R / −13% C; bear −26% R / −32% C. Change after trend ends +53% (bull) / +48% (bear). Pullbacks occur 62% (bull) / 65% (bear) and hurt performance: with pullback 18%/24% vs. without 21%/32%. Performance with breakout gap 20% (bull) / 33% (bear). Busted pattern 39% (bull) / 31% (bear). Days to ultimate low 68 (bull) / 39 (bear). Dual bumps 28% (bull) / 38% (bear). Performance rank 3 of 21 (bull) / 4 of 21 (bear). Volume trend: upward. Best breakouts occur in the middle of the yearly price range. Tall patterns outperform short ones.

**Trading tactics:** Draw a warning line one lead-in height above the trend line — entering the sell zone. Draw a sell line one lead-in height above the warning line — confirms a BARR. Consider selling when price touches the sell line, especially if the bump is narrow; delay if the bump is rounded and price continues up. If the company, industry, or market looks weak, take profits on the touch. After the close below the trend line, sell immediately in a bear market because declines are steep. In a bear market, falling volume trend signals better postbreakout performance. Avoid short-and-wide patterns; tall-and-narrow or tall-and-wide perform best.

## Cup with Handle

**Type:** Short-term bullish continuation. Appears in an uptrend following a prior rise of at least 30% into the left cup lip.

**Identification:** Cup profile with a handle on the right. Use arithmetic scale. Require a 30%+ rise before the cup. U-shaped (not V) cup lasting 7–65 weeks. Handle of at least 1 week (5 trading days) and at least 7 days; handle forms in the upper half of the cup; volume trends downward into the handle. Cup lips (left and right highs) should be at approximately the same price level. Volume surges on the breakout. Breakout = close above the right cup lip. Patterns with short handles and a higher left cup lip outperform.

**Psychology:** Profit-taking after the prior uptrend creates the rounded cup; the handle represents a brief consolidation where supply is absorbed before demand returns. When prices clear the right cup lip on rising volume, the uptrend resumes, and the stock typically throws back to the lip before continuing higher.

**Measure rule:** Compute formation height = lowest low in the cup minus high at the right cup lip (added positively). Add this height to the right cup lip for a target met 50% of the time in bull markets (27% in bear). For a more reliable target, use half the cup height added to the right cup lip — reached 76% of the time in bull and 55% in bear.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 5% (bull) / 7% (bear). Average rise 34% (bull) / 23% (bear). R/C performance: bull 15% R / 35% C; bear 0% R / 23% C. Rises over 45% occur 28% of the time in bull, 14% in bear. Change after trend ends −30% (bull) / −34% (bear). Throwbacks 58% (bull) / 42% (bear); with throwback 30%/25% rise vs. without 40%/21%. Performance with breakout gap 39% (bull) / 20% (bear). Days to ultimate high 167 (bull) / 63 (bear). Average formation length 166 days (bull) / 151 days (bear). Tall patterns 37% (bull) / 26% (bear) vs. short 32%/20%. Short handle 37% (bull) / 23% (bear) vs. long handle 32%/20%. Higher left cup lip performs best: 35% (bull) / 29% (bear). U-shaped volume 38% (bull) / 28% (bear). Heavy breakout volume 34% (bull) / 25% (bear); light 37%/17%. Performance rank 13 of 23 (bull) / 9 of 19 (bear). Volume trend: downward (bull) / upward (bear). Best breakouts near the yearly low.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for the breakout — buy when price closes above the right cup lip. For an inner cup within the pattern, buy the inner breakout and prepare to sell at the old (outer) high. Place a stop 0.15 below the handle low; raise the stop to breakeven or just below the nearest support zone as price rises, and continue trailing it up. Prefer tall cups with short handles and a higher left cup lip; U-shaped volume patterns perform best; bull-market breakouts near the yearly low are favored.

## Cup with Handle, Inverted

**Type:** Short-term bearish reversal. Appears as a price top (reversal) or as a continuation of a downtrend.

**Identification:** Inverted (rounded) cup with handle on the right. Use arithmetic scale. Look for a smooth rounded cup; the two cup rims (lows on each side) should be within 6% of each other. Handle = price action from the right rim to the breakout; median handle length 40 days. Handle must show a bounce upward but stay below the cup top. Most common retraces within the handle are 42%, 35%, and 60% (near Fibonacci 50%, 38%, and 62%). Breakout = close below the right cup rim low. Watch for a head-and-shoulders top with a fat head and handle-shaped shoulders.

**Psychology:** Buying demand exhausts at the rounded top, supply takes over, and the handle becomes a corrective bounce before the downtrend resumes. The downward breakout confirms that supply has overwhelmed demand.

**Measure rule:** Compute the handle height (handle peak minus the right rim low), then subtract it from the right rim low to get the target price. This target is reached 47% of the time in bull markets and 49% in bear markets; the traditional cup-height method succeeds only 12–15% of the time and is unreliable.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 11% (bull) / 2% (bear). Average decline 16% (bull) / 26% (bear). R/C performance: bull 16% R / 16% C; bear 26% R / 26% C. Declines over 45% occur 2% of the time in bull, 6% in bear. Change after trend ends +56% (bull) / +54% (bear). Pullbacks 54% (bull) / 48% (bear); with pullback 14%/23% vs. without 19%/29%. Performance with breakout gap −18% (bull) / −27% (bear). Busted pattern 62% (bull) / 65% (bear). Days to ultimate low 53 (bull) / 27 (bear). Average formation length 118 days (bull) / 145 days (bear). Tall patterns −18% (bull) / −29% (bear) vs. short −15%/−23%. Narrow −17% (bull) / −27% (bear) vs. wide −15%/−25%. Short-and-wide perform worst; tall-and-narrow perform best. Even rims outperform uneven rims. Performance rank 8 of 21 (bull) / 1 of 21 (bear). Volume trend: downward. Volume is generally a poor predictor of performance. Best breakouts near the yearly low (bull) or middle of the range (bear).

**Trading tactics:** Open a short when price closes below the right rim low and always use a stop. If the decline is sharp and nearly vertical over several days, cover the short — prices usually rebound after such drops. Alternatively, draw a down-sloping trend line from the handle and cover when price closes above it. Check for nearby support before entering because pullbacks hurt performance. Look for a possible measured-move-down with the handle as the corrective phase. Trade in a bear market for the largest declines and lowest failure rates; select tall or narrow patterns, prefer even rims, and watch for a trend change six to seven weeks after the breakout.
