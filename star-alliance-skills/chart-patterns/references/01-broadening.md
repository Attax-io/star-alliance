# Broadening Patterns

## Broadening Bottoms

**Type:** Upward breakout = short-term bullish reversal; downward breakout = short-term bearish continuation. Preceded by a downward price trend (the trend differentiates it from a Broadening Top, an arbitrary distinction).

**Identification:** Megaphone shape with higher highs and lower lows widening over time. Top trend line slopes up; bottom slopes down (opposing slopes distinguish it from right-angled or same-direction variants). Needs at least two minor highs and two minor lows (not necessarily alternating). Volume usually trends upward, often in a U-shape. Breakout is upward when price closes above the formation's high or downward when price closes below the pattern's low. Premature breakouts are rare. A five-point reversal (three minor highs, two minor lows) is a rare subtype.

**Psychology:** Chaos-like oscillation — small disturbances amplify; price hits a new high, then crosses over to make a new low, with each successive swing exceeding the prior one, reflecting increasing indecision and volatility.

**Measure rule:** Height = highest high minus lowest low. For upward breakouts, add the height to the highest high. For downward breakouts, subtract the height from the lowest low. Use the highest minor high (drawn horizontally forward) as the actual breakout price when price rides along the trend line without piercing it.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 10% (bull/up), 9% (bear/up), 16% (bull/down), 9% (bear/down). Average rise 27% (bull), 21% (bear); average decline 15% (bull), 18% (bear). Throwbacks 41% / 44%; pullbacks 42% / 56%. Change after trend ends: –34% / –35% after upward breakouts; +51% / +46% after downward breakouts. Meets price target 59% / 53% (up), 44% / 31% (down). Busted-pattern performance: +51% (bull), –26% (bear, down). Days to ultimate high/low: 112 (bull/up), 65 (bear/up), 40 (bull/down), 24 (bear/down). Below-average overall performance.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for a confirmed close outside the trend line. Go long after price bounces off the lower trend line (two minor highs/lows required first); sell when price turns down. Place a long stop 0.15 below the minor low and ratchet it up to 0.15 below each prior minor low as price makes new highs. Go short after price turns down from the top trend line; place a stop 0.15 above the highest high. For downward breakouts, cover near the target or any support level. Use partial declines to go long and partial rises to short. Avoid stocks in a dead-cat bounce. For best performance: trade with the market trend (bull/up or bear/down); select tall or narrow patterns (tall-and-narrow is the top combination); prefer a falling volume trend for upward breakouts and a rising volume trend for downward breakouts; a random volume shape (flat, rising, or falling) outperforms U- or dome-shaped; avoid BBs in bull markets with downward breakouts (highest failure rates); expect a possible trend change 5–6 weeks after the breakout.

## Broadening Formations, Right-Angled and Ascending

**Type:** Upward breakout = short-term bearish reversal; downward breakout = short-term bearish continuation. Despite the "ascending" label, the formation is bearish.

**Identification:** Megaphone with a horizontal bottom and an up-sloping top trend line. The top line must have at least two minor highs; the bottom at least two minor lows. Volume trends upward with a slight U-shape tendency, especially in the final third. Premature breakouts are very rare — a close below the horizontal trend line is most likely genuine. Downward breakouts predominate and are usually accompanied by a volume surge.

**Psychology:** Investors perceive value at a fixed price (the horizontal base) and buy, pushing price higher; each rally meets mounting selling pressure until the buyers at the base are exhausted, the support collapses, and price punches through the base downward. The broadening top range reflects an ever-rising minimum price sellers demand.

**Measure rule:** Height = highest high minus the horizontal trend line. For upward breakouts, add the height to the highest high. For downward breakouts, subtract the height from the horizontal trend line. A more conservative (and accurate) approach uses half the formation height.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 11% / 11% (up), 20% / 8% (down). Average rise 29% (bull) / 15% (bear); average decline 15% / 22%. Throwbacks 47% / 43%; pullbacks 65% / 52%. Change after trend ends: –31% / –38% (up), +53% / +47% (down). Meets price target 68% / 43% (up), 32% / 51% (down). Busted-pattern performance: +41% (bull/up), –25% to –40% (down). Days to ultimate high/low: 131 (bull/up), 39 (bear/up), 47 (bull/down), 42 (bear/down).

**Trading tactics:** Wait for a confirmed close below the lower trend line before shorting — premature breakouts do occur. Use a partial rise or decline as an early entry signal (correctly predicts breakout direction 74%+ of the time); consider buying on a close above the halfway point across the formation. Sell a long position as soon as price closes below the horizontal trend line. For tall patterns, consider intraformation trading: buy at the lower trend line, sell at the top, ratcheting stops to just below the prior minor low as price rises. For best performance: trade with the market trend; select tall patterns, especially tall-and-wide (avoid short-and-wide); use a falling volume trend in a bull market and a rising volume trend in a bear market; dome-shaped volume (except bull/down where U-shape is better); heavy breakout volume except in bear/down breakouts; in bull/up, let profits run (long rise), but in bear/down use a profit target (steep, short decline); expect trend weakness about 6–7 weeks after a bull-market breakout.

## Broadening Formations, Right-Angled and Descending

**Type:** Upward breakout = short-term bullish continuation; downward breakout = short-term bearish reversal. Appears after a downward price trend (mirror of the right-angled ascending).

**Identification:** Megaphone tilted down with a horizontal top trend line and a down-sloping bottom. Top line requires at least two minor highs at the same (or nearly same) price; bottom requires at least two minor lows. Volume tends upward, often dome-shaped. Premature breakouts are rare. Breakout can be in either direction; trend lines projected forward frequently act as support/resistance for months or years.

**Psychology:** A fixed-price block of supply at the top of the formation matches eager buyers; once that supply is exhausted, price rises — but if supply overwhelms buyers, price declines through the lower trend line. Each successive test of the top meets fewer willing sellers, but also thinner demand.

**Measure rule:** Height = highest high minus lowest low. For upward breakouts, add the height to the value of the horizontal top trend line. For downward breakouts, subtract the height from the lowest low.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 19% / 6% (up), 14% / 4% (down). Average rise 28% (bull) / 23% (bear); average decline 15% / 23%. Throwbacks 52% / 50%; pullbacks 51% / 57%. Change after trend ends: –26% / –35% (up), +55% / +55% (down). Meets price target 63% / 42% (up), 44% / 51% (down). Busted-pattern performance: +44% / +35% (up), –26% / –55% (down). Days to ultimate high/low: 146 (bull/up), 67 (bear/up), 45 (bull/down), 41 (bear/down). Below-average overall performance.

**Trading tactics:** Always wait for a confirmed close outside the trend lines before trading. Use a partial rise that curls back down as a short entry in a bear market (correctly predicts a downward breakout 80% of the time). Once a breakout occurs, treat the opposite side of the formation as the initial stop-loss reference, but look for nearer support or resistance; ratchet the stop toward break-even and beyond as the trade progresses. For tall patterns, consider intraformation trading: long at the lower trend line, short at the top. For best performance: trade with the market trend; after a downward breakout, buy the stock once the trend changes (rebound averages 55%); select tall patterns; in a bull market, prefer breakouts in the middle of the yearly price range; in a bear market, prefer breakouts near the yearly low; rising volume trend is usually best; U-shaped volume for upward breakouts, dome-shaped for downward; heavy breakout volume usually helps; throwbacks and pullbacks hurt performance; expect a possible trend change about a month after an upward breakout in a bear market.

## Broadening Tops

**Type:** Upward breakout = short-term bullish continuation; downward breakout = short-term bearish reversal. Preceded by an upward price trend (mirror of the Broadening Bottom). Synonyms: expanding triangle, orthodox broadening top, five-point reversal.

**Identification:** Megaphone with higher highs and lower lows. Top trend line slopes up, bottom slopes down (opposing slopes). At least two minor highs and two minor lows (not necessarily alternating). Volume trends upward, often U-shaped. Breakout in either direction when price closes outside the trend lines; prices may move horizontally for months before a definitive breakout. A five-point reversal has three peaks and two troughs.

**Psychology:** A push-pull between buyers and sellers — buyers treat each dip as oversold and step in; sellers unload into each rally to lock in profits, broadening the swings until one side wins.

**Measure rule:** Height = highest high minus lowest low. For upward breakouts, add the height to the highest high. For downward breakouts, subtract the height from the lowest low. The breakout price itself is the highest peak (for upward) or the lowest trough (for downward) of the formation.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 15% / 11% (up), 18% / 3% (down). Average rise 29% (bull) / 24% (bear); average decline 15% / 20%. Throwbacks 54% / 53%; pullbacks 48% / 62%. Change after trend ends: –33% / –33% (up), +53% / +49% (down). Meets price target 62% / 61% (up), 37% / 32% (down). Busted-pattern performance: +40% / +42% (up), –33% / –27% (down). Days to ultimate high/low: 116 (bull/up), 81 (bear/up), 50 (bull/down), 29 (bear/down). Poor performer versus the all-pattern average.

**Trading tactics:** Buy after the stock turns up from the lower trend line, with a stop 0.15 below the minor low; sell short after price turns down from the top trend line, with a stop 0.15 above the minor high. Ratchet stops to the next minor low/high as price makes new extremes. A partial decline is a buy trigger; a partial rise is a sell trigger (correctly predicts breakout direction over 70% of the time). For best performance: trade with the market trend; select bull-market patterns for the lowest failure rates on extended moves; pick patterns with breakouts near the yearly low; U-shaped volume is best for most patterns (dome-shaped for bull/up); light breakout volume usually helps; tall patterns outperform; in a bull market, expect a long rise (be patient); in a bear market, half of downward breakouts bottom in about two weeks — be ready to take profits quickly; expect a possible trend change 4–6 weeks after a bear-market breakout.

## Broadening Wedges, Ascending

**Type:** Upward breakout = short-term bullish continuation; downward breakout = short-term bearish reversal. Most often appears at the end of a rising price trend. Rare overall (255 samples in the study).

**Identification:** Megaphone tilted up — both trend lines slope upward, with the top line having a steeper slope than the bottom. Neither trend line is horizontal. Requires at least three distinct touches (or near-touches) of each trend line. Volume is irregular with a slight upward tendency. Premature breakouts are very rare. Breaks out downward about 73% of the time. A partial rise (price touches the lower line, climbs but fails to reach the upper line, then returns to the lower line) reliably precedes a downward breakout.

**Psychology:** Concentrated buying enthusiasm (a large buyer, share buyback, momentum players) pushes price to new highs, but value sellers unload at each peak; buyers step in at progressively higher "value" levels, but eventually the demand collapses (often on negative news) and price drops through the lower support.

**Measure rule:** For downward breakouts, use the lowest price in the formation as the minimum expected price move (this works 58–86% of the time). For upward breakouts, compute the formation height (highest high minus lowest low) and add it to the breakout price.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 2% (bull/up), 0% (bear/up), 11% (bull/down), 14% (bear/down). Average rise 38% (bull) / 18% (bear); average decline 17% / 21%. Throwbacks 50% / 70%; pullbacks 57% / 52%. Change after trend ends: –31% / –30% (up), +49% / +37% (down). Meets price target 69% / 60% (up), 58% / 86% (down). Days to ultimate high/low: 161 (bull/up), 78 (bear/up), 63 (bull/down), 51 (bear/down). The 38% bull-market average rise is above the 36% average for all bullish patterns.

**Trading tactics:** Although 73% break out downward, wait for a confirmed breakout before shorting. If a partial rise appears and price curls back toward the lower trend line, consider shorting in advance (74–78% of partial rises are followed by a downward breakout); move the stop to break-even as price declines to the lower trend line. Cover the short as price nears the target or a support level. For best performance: trade with the market trend (bear/down or bull/up); bull/up has the lowest failure rates; select tall or narrow patterns; heavy breakout volume; throwbacks and pullbacks hurt performance, but breakout day gaps usually help; expect a possible trend change about 6 weeks after a bull-market breakout.

## Broadening Wedges, Descending

**Type:** Upward breakout = short-term bullish reversal; downward breakout = bearish (evenly split between reversal and continuation, slightly more often a reversal).

**Identification:** Megaphone tilted down — both trend lines slope downward, with the bottom line having a steeper slope, so the formation broadens over time. Neither line is horizontal. At least two distinct touches of each trend line. Volume usually rises over the life of the formation. Breakout can be in either direction; 55% of the time price breaks out opposite the prevailing trend. A partial decline (price touches the top line, drops, turns up without reaching the lower line) usually precedes an upward breakout.

**Psychology:** Supply and demand oscillate with growing amplitude as buyers and sellers repeatedly test resolve at successively lower extremes; eventually a catalyst (e.g., favorable news, exhausted supply) lets one side win decisively.

**Measure rule:** For upward breakouts, the price target is the highest high in the pattern (reached 79% bull / 58% bear). For downward breakouts, subtract the formation height from the lowest low (works only 32–36% of the time — look instead for underlying support).

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 6% / 11% (up), 9% / 2% (down). Average rise 33% (bull) / 24% (bear); average decline 20% / 25%. Throwbacks 53% / 61%; pullbacks 53% / 66%. Change after trend ends: –33% / –32% (up), +47% / +49% (down). Meets price target 79% / 58% (up), 36% / 32% (down). Busted-pattern performance: +24% / +43% (up), –20% / –25% (down). Days to ultimate high/low: 131 (bull/up), 66 (bear/up), 40 (bull/down), 23 (bear/down). Strong performer on downward breakouts versus the all-pattern averages.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for a confirmed close beyond the trend lines before trading. If a partial decline appears (price drops from the top trend line, curls up, and breaks out upward), consider going long early; place a stop 0.15 below the curl low. For broad formations, consider intraformation trades: buy at the lower trend line and sell at the top, or short at the top and cover near the bottom; place a stop 0.15 beyond the relevant trend line. Ratchet stops as price moves in your favor, using nearby support or resistance. For best performance: trade with the market trend (bull/up or bear/down); bull/up has the lowest failure rates for extended moves; bear markets throw back or pull back more often, but bull-market breakouts are stronger; throwbacks/pullbacks hurt performance but breakout day gaps help; a partial decline in a bull market correctly predicts an upward breakout 87% of the time and improves performance; expect a possible trend change 5–7 weeks after the breakout; select tall-and-narrow patterns and avoid short-and-wide; falling volume trend for upward breakouts, rising for downward; U-shaped volume usually best; heavy breakout volume helps in bull markets (light volume slightly better in bear markets).
