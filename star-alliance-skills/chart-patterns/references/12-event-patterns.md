# Event Patterns (earnings, dead-cat bounce, FDA, sales, ratings)

## Dead-Cat Bounce

**Type:** Short-term bearish reversal; bearish; appears after dramatic decline in any prevailing trend.

**Identification:** Three-phase pattern. (1) Event phase: price gaps down and plunges typically 25%–45% (range 15%–78%), usually 1–2 days, high volume (often several times normal); 74%–81% begin with a price gap. (2) Bounce: price recovers 15%–35% over 5–25 days, peaking roughly 3 weeks after the event low. (3) Postbounce decline: price drops 15%–45% over 10–50 days, with over 75% falling below the event low.

**Psychology:** A negative event catches holders by surprise; panic selling drives the plunge. Novice buyers step in on the bounce, but the underlying problem (earnings, sales, fraud) typically persists, and stocks give back the bounce and more. One DCB often follows another because management cannot fix severe problems in a quarter.

**Measure rule:** No single formula stated. Use postbounce decline of 18% (bull) or 27% (bear) below the event low as a downside target, or 38% (bull) / 48% (bear) decline from the close the day before the event to the postbounce low as a worst-case benchmark.

**Key numbers:** Event decline 31% bull / 35% bear. Bounce 28% bull / 35% bear. Postbounce decline 30% bull / 40% bear. ~75% make a low below the event low after the bounce. 38%–61% have another 15%+ decline within 6 months (more in bear market). About half of event-day gaps close within 6 months; the rest do not. Large event losses produce larger but slower bounces than small event losses.

**Trading tactics:** If long, ride the bounce and sell when price rounds over at the top or breaks an up-sloping minor-low trend line. If short, expect the bounce, then ride the postbounce decline. For swing traders, buy near the event low and sell at the bounce high. Do not trade any bullish chart pattern within 6 months of a DCB. Trade with the trend: declines are faster and steeper in a bear market. Use Tables 54.6 and 54.7 to time entry and exit based on typical phase durations and price ranges.

## Dead-Cat Bounce, Inverted

**Type:** Short-term bearish reversal; bearish; appears after a large one-day upward event-driven move.

**Identification:** Stock jumps 5%–20%+ in a single day (often on a positive earnings surprise or similar catalyst, sometimes gapping up on heavy volume), then gives back most of the gain over the following days. Day 2 typically posts the highest high of the next month.

**Psychology:** "Buy on the rumor, sell on the news." After a sharp rally, profit-taking and disappointed follow-through drive the stock back toward or below the launch point.

**Measure rule:** None stated. Use price-profiles (Figures 55.2–55.5) to time entry and exit.

**Key numbers:** For 5% moves, 49% of patterns show a higher close on day 2; for 10% moves, 48% make higher highs on day 2; for 15% moves, 46% make higher highs on day 2 (drops to 40% on day 3); for 20% moves, 45% on day 2 (drops to 37% on days 3–4). By month's end, about half of the patterns have higher highs than day 1.

**Trading tactics:** Sell the day after the initial rise. A limit order at the day-1 intraday high is one approach. If shorting, set stops. For larger moves (15%–20%), consider buying back near the day-3 intraday low and selling into weeks 2 or 4. For 5%–10% moves, do not buy back in—price tends to trend lower. Take profits when a stock jumps 5%+; large single-day rises are typically followed by declines.

## Earnings Surprise, Bad

**Type:** Short-term bearish continuation; bearish; appears in a falling price trend.

**Identification:** Quarterly earnings announcement with a downward breakout (price closes below the intraday low posted on the announcement day). The intraday trading range on the announcement day is wider than the 1-month average; high volume is typical. A bad quarter usually follows a bad quarter (74% same-direction breakouts quarter to quarter).

**Psychology:** Disappointing earnings confirm company trouble; selling pressure builds and the existing downtrend extends. Sometimes insiders buy or the company announces a buyback, halting the decline.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the intraday low from the intraday high on the announcement day, then subtract that difference from the intraday low. The result is the target price. Target is met 69% (bull) / 68% (bear) of the time.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 31% bull / 26% bear. Average decline 13% bull / 17% bear. Change after trend ends 51% bull / 37% bear (rebound). Pullbacks 41% bull / 45% bear. About half reach the ultimate low within a week. Patterns with the announcement day range 2–3× the 1-month average outperform. Best performance near the yearly low (averages 14% bull / 20% bear).

**Trading tactics:** Wait for confirmation (close below the intraday low) before trading. If long, consider selling to limit loss or ride the bounce and exit before the postbounce decline. If shorting, enter on the close below the pattern low; use stops. Trade in a bear market and in a downward price trend for best results. Avoid pullbacks (they hurt performance). Tall patterns (above-median announcement range) perform better. Half the patterns bottom in a week, so plan for quick exits.

## Earnings Surprise, Good

**Type:** Short-term bullish continuation; bullish; appears in a rising price trend.

**Identification:** Quarterly earnings announcement with an upward breakout (price closes above the intraday high posted on the announcement day). Intraday trading range on the announcement day is larger than the 1-month average; volume is heavy. A large trading range (2–3× average) improves performance and lowers failure rate.

**Psychology:** Good earnings surprise attracts buyers; price rises, but often the run is short-lived as the "buy on the rumor, sell on the news" effect kicks in. Many patterns throw back to the breakout price.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the intraday low from the intraday high on the announcement day, then add the difference to the intraday high. The result is the target price. Target is met 76% (bull) / 74% (bear) of the time.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure rate 29% bull / 28% bear. Average rise 24% bull / 14% bear. Change after trend ends −27% bull / −31% bear (decline from ultimate high). Throwbacks 41% bull / 44% bear. Busted pattern performance −22% bull / −27% bear. Almost half fail to rise more than 10%. Days to ultimate high 69 bull / 40 bear. Best performance when breakout is near the yearly low (30% bull / 19% bear).

**Trading tactics:** Never trade ahead of an earnings announcement. Buy when price closes above the announcement day's high. Trade with the trend, in a rising price trend and a bull market, with other stocks in the industry also rising. Place a limit order near the target price for swing trades. Tall patterns (above-median range) perform better. Throwbacks hurt performance, so avoid patterns with nearby overhead resistance. Expect a brief rise; half top out in the first week or two.

## FDA Drug Approvals

**Type:** Upward breakout: short-term bullish continuation. Downward breakout: short-term bearish reversal.

**Identification:** News announcement of an FDA drug approval. A large price move (intraday range wider than the 1-month average) or a price gap on the announcement day. Breakout occurs when price closes above the intraday high (upward) or below the intraday low (downward) on the announcement day; typically within 4 days. Volume is usually heavy. About half of approvals result in downward breakouts.

**Psychology:** "Buy on the rumor, sell on the news." Anticipation of approval already drives price up; the actual announcement often disappoints or signals that profits are still distant. Even after an upward breakout, 42% are trending down within a week.

**Measure rule:** For upward breakouts, subtract the intraday low from the high and add the result to the intraday high. For downward breakouts, subtract the result from the intraday low. Target is met 81% (upward) / 78% (downward) of the time.

**Key numbers:** Upward: break-even failure 34%, average rise 20%, change after trend ends −29%, throwbacks 61%, days to ultimate high 105. Downward: break-even failure 39%, average decline 13%, change after trend ends +52% (rise), pullbacks 66%, days to ultimate low 30. About half the patterns reach the ultimate high or low in the first week. Reversals outperform continuations. Inverted J-shape patterns (peak 3–6 weeks after breakout, followed by trend-line pierce) show a 18% decline from pierce to ultimate low (informal study, 20 samples). Heavy announcement-day volume improves performance for both directions.

**Trading tactics:** Avoid trading unless the intraday range is unusually wide (2–3× the 1-month average). Look for the inverted J-shape: an up move, a peak 3–6 weeks after breakout, then a trend-line pierce for the sell/short signal. For downward breakouts, buy after the stock bottoms and rides the post-trend rise (averages 52%). Busted patterns are tradable. Take profits quickly—many patterns peak or bottom in the first week.

## Flag, Earnings

**Type:** Short-term bullish continuation; bullish; appears after a good earnings announcement, in a rising price trend.

**Identification:** (1) A surprisingly good earnings announcement sends the stock sharply higher, often in a near-vertical flagpole run. (2) A consolidation region (flag, pennant, or irregular shape) follows, usually sloping downward or horizontal. (3) Breakout above the highest high of the pattern (confirmation price) or above a flag trend line. Volume typically trends downward through the formation. Best performed in a bull market with the price in an uptrend.

**Psychology:** Earnings surprise generates buying excitement; the flag represents a brief pause and digestion before the uptrend resumes. Often the run is interrupted by a throwback to the breakout price.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the announcement-day low from the formation high; add the result to the flag low (lowest low to the right of the flagpole). Target is met 86% (bull) / 84% (bear) of the time.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure 10% bull / 16% bear. Average rise 34% bull / 22% bear. Change after trend ends −33% bull / −32% bear (decline from ultimate high). Throwbacks 63% bull / 58% bear. Busted pattern performance −30% bull / −28% bear. Best performance when breakout is near the yearly low (48% bull / 30% bear). Tall flags (height > ~14% of breakout price) outperform short flags. Tall and narrow flags perform best. Light breakout volume outperforms heavy. 47% (bull) / 48% (bear) reach ultimate high in the first week. Flagpole-top to breakout averages 16–18 days.

**Trading tactics:** Buy when price closes above the formation high or pierces a flag trend line. Avoid earnings flags in a downward price trend (act as continuations). Beware area gaps that close quickly. Watch for a throwback within 11–12 days; if price curls over within a week, take profits. Raise stops as price climbs. Tall and narrow patterns are best. Light breakout volume is preferable. The pattern often appears near the yearly high, but the best performance comes from flags near the yearly low.

## Same-Store Sales, Bad

**Type:** Short-term bearish continuation; bearish; appears in a downtrend.

**Identification:** A retailer announces monthly or quarterly same-store sales numbers interpreted as bad. A large downward price move (intraday range wider than the 1-month average) or a downward price gap the day of or the day after the announcement. Downward breakout: price closes below the intraday low posted on the announcement day. Volume is heavy. One 3S pattern often follows another.

**Psychology:** Weak same-store sales suggest the chain cannot grow organically; the announcement confirms trader suspicions and accelerates the existing downtrend. The bounce that follows is a dead-cat bounce in miniature.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the intraday low from the intraday high on the announcement day; subtract the result from the intraday low. Target is met 68% (bull) / 69% (bear) of the time.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure 26% bull / 27% bear. Average decline 12% bull / 14% bear. Change after trend ends +54% bull / +39% bear (rebound from low). Pullbacks 53% bull / 60% bear. Just 1% decline more than 45%. Half reach the ultimate low in a week. Best performance when breakout is in the middle of the yearly price range (14% bull / 16% bear). Tall patterns outperform short ones. Heavy announcement-day volume slightly helps in bear markets.

**Trading tactics:** Sell long holdings or short on the close below the formation low. Use stops. For best performance, trade in a bear market, look for patterns with wide intraday range, and check for nearby support that might trigger a pullback. Pullbacks hurt performance. Cover the short or buy long once price bottoms, riding the post-trend rebound. One 3S pattern often follows another, so watch for repeat announcements.

## Same-Store Sales, Good

**Type:** Short-term bullish continuation; bullish; appears in a rising price trend.

**Identification:** A retailer announces monthly or quarterly same-store sales numbers interpreted as good. A large upward price move (intraday range wider than the 1-month average) or an upward price gap the day of or the day after the announcement. Upward breakout: price closes above the intraday high posted on the announcement day. Volume is heavy. A large trading range (2–3× average) improves performance and lowers failure rate.

**Psychology:** Better-than-expected same-store sales boost the stock, but the rise is often brief. Insiders frequently sell at the post-announcement peak, and price tumbles within weeks. "Buy on the rumor, sell on the news" is a recurring theme.

**Measure rule:** Subtract the intraday low from the intraday high on the announcement day; add the result to the intraday high. Target is met 82% (bull) / 72% (bear) of the time.

**Key numbers:** Break-even failure 20% bull / 27% bear. Average rise 23% bull / 14% bear. Change after trend ends −28% bull / −31% bear (decline from ultimate high). Busted pattern performance −20% bull / −27% bear. Throwbacks 59% bull / 70% bear. Days to ultimate high 64 bull / 41 bear. Best performance when breakout is near the yearly low (33% bull / 26% bear). Tall patterns outperform (30% bull / 18% bear versus 17% bull / 11% bear for short). About 44% (bear) / 36% (bull) of patterns top out in the first week.

**Trading tactics:** Buy when price closes above the announcement day's intraday high. Place a stop-loss order to protect profits. Sell within 1–2 weeks as price rounds over—the ultimate high often comes quickly. Avoid bear markets (27% break-even failure). In a bull market, reversals outperform continuations. Throwbacks hurt performance, so avoid patterns with nearby overhead resistance. Once price rolls over, consider shorting with stops. Tall patterns are best. A common setup is gap-up, brief rise, throwback to gap support, then renewed decline.

## Stock Downgrades

**Type:** Upward breakout: short-term bullish reversal. Downward breakout: short-term bearish continuation.

**Identification:** A major brokerage firm announces a downgrade and the news reaches the financial press. Intraday trading range on the announcement day (or the next) is wider than the 1-month average, or price gaps when trading resumes. Breakout occurs when price closes above the intraday high (upward, ~39% of cases) or below the intraday low (downward, ~61%). Volume is heavy. Most breakouts occur near the yearly low.

**Psychology:** A downgrade typically confirms existing bearish sentiment, but the announcement frequently arrives near a price low where downside is already exhausted. In upward breakouts, fear overcomes greed and price eventually tumbles. In downward breakouts, price often drops for a week or two and then recovers.

**Measure rule:** For upward breakouts, subtract the intraday low from the high and add the result to the intraday high. For downward breakouts, subtract the result from the intraday low. Target is met 64%–71% of the time depending on direction and market.

**Key numbers:** Upward breakout: break-even failure 25% bull / 28% bear; average rise 27% bull / 14% bear; change after trend ends −30% bull / −34% bear; throwbacks 49% bull / 50% bear; reverses average 36% bull / 21% bear; days to ultimate high 79 bull / 27 bear. Downward breakout: break-even failure 26% bull / 17% bear; average decline 14% bull / 19% bear; change after trend ends +50% bull / +35% bear; pullbacks 48% bull / 45% bear; busted pattern performance −21% bull / −34% bear; days to ultimate low 26 bull / 24 bear. About half the patterns reach the ultimate high or low within 1–2 weeks. Best performance near the yearly low. Tall patterns outperform short ones.

**Trading tactics:** Do not buy on the announcement. If long, sell immediately on a downward breakout or wait for a brief pullback to sell. For downward breakouts, the best short candidates are in a bear market; use stops because downgrades often appear near the bottom. For upward breakouts, watch price climb 1–3 weeks then round over—that is the shorting opportunity. Always wait for confirmation (close above the high or below the low). Tall patterns with wide intraday range are best. Pullbacks and throwbacks generally hurt performance.

## Stock Upgrades

**Type:** Upward breakout: short-term bullish continuation. Downward breakout: short-term bearish continuation.

**Identification:** A major brokerage firm announces an upgrade (or addition to a recommended list) and the news reaches the financial press. Intraday trading range on the announcement day (or the next) is wider than the 1-month average, or price gaps. Breakout: close above the intraday high (upward) or below the intraday low (downward). About 35% of upgrades result in downward breakouts. Volume is heavy. Upward breakouts often occur near the yearly high.

**Psychology:** "Buy on the rumor, sell on the news." Upgrades frequently appear at or near peaks, and the bullish move is short-lived before a sharp reversal. After an upward breakout, nearly half of patterns decline substantially within 2 weeks. After a downward breakout, half surge upward within a week.

**Measure rule:** For upward breakouts, subtract the intraday low from the high and add the result to the intraday high. For downward breakouts, subtract the result from the intraday low. Target is met 67%–81% of the time depending on direction and market.

**Key numbers:** Upward breakout: break-even failure 18% bull / 21% bear; average rise 24% bull / 16% bear; change after trend ends −30% bull / −32% bear; throwbacks 63% bull / 59% bear; busted pattern performance 38% bull / 16% bear; days to ultimate high 61 bull / 43 bear. Downward breakout: break-even failure 38% bull / 23% bear; average decline 12% bull / 18% bear; change after trend ends +44% bull / +35% bear; pullbacks 37% bull / 50% bear; busted pattern performance −22% bull / −30% bear; days to ultimate low 25 bull / 25 bear. Tall patterns outperform (29% bull / 22% bear versus 20% bull / 11% bear for short). Continuation breakouts outperform reversals. About 50% (bull) / 53% (bull, down) reach the ultimate high or low in the first week. Best performance: upward breakouts in bull markets near the yearly high; downward breakouts near the yearly low.

**Trading tactics:** Wait for confirmation. For upward breakouts, hold existing longs, then consider selling or shorting as price rounds over within 1–3 weeks; use stops. For downward breakouts, buy once price confirms it has bottomed and is recovering; cover shorts or buy on weakness. Upgrades near the yearly high in a bull market are best for longs. Avoid downward breakouts in bull markets (highest failure rates). Tall patterns with wide intraday range and heavy announcement-day volume are best. Busted patterns are tradable.
