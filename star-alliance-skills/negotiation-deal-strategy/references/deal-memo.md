# Negotiation plan & deal memo templates

Two artifacts, one boundary. The **negotiation plan** is written *before* the table; the **deal memo** is written *after* a conversation to capture where things stand and recommend the next move. Both end at the Guild Master's desk — this skill drafts and recommends, it never signs, accepts, or commits.

## Advisory boundary (put this at the top of every artifact)

> This document is advisory. It recommends a negotiating position and next move. It does **not** constitute acceptance of any term, an offer, or a commitment. No term is binding until the Guild Master approves it and binding language is executed via `legal-drafting` / `contract-review`.

## Writing notes

- **Lead with the recommendation**, then show the reasoning — the Guild Master reads the ask first.
- **Separate agreed-in-principle from binding.** A verbal "yes on the shape" is not a signed term; label it as such so nobody treats the memo as a contract.
- **Always end with the decision left to the human.** The memo's job is to tee up a clean decision, not to make it.
- **Keep the proof and the math.** Show the BATNA, the ZOPA, and the comparables behind every recommended number so the Guild Master can sanity-check, not just trust.
