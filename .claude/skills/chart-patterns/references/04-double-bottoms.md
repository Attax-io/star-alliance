# Double Bottoms (Adam & Eve variants)

## Double Bottoms, Adam & Adam (AADB)

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal; forms at the end of a downward price trend (or as the corrective phase of a measured move up). All act as reversals of the downtrend by definition.

**Identification:**
- Two narrow, V-shaped bottoms (sometimes single one- or two-day downward spikes), looking similar to each other.
- Price trends downward into the pattern and should not drift below the left bottom.
- Rise between bottoms: at least 10% from the lowest valley to the highest peak between the two bottoms; taller patterns perform better.
- Bottom-to-bottom price variation should be small; best performance with 2%–5% variation. A lower right bottom is preferred.
- Bottom separation: best 3–6 weeks; performance deteriorates after 8 weeks.
- Price must close above the confirmation point (the highest high between the two bottoms) without first falling below the right bottom low; this close is the breakout.
- Volume usually higher on the left bottom than the right; falling volume trend is preferred.

**Psychology:** A double bottom is a retest of the low. Investors buy the first bottom hoping the decline has ended, but the rally fails and prices return to retest. If the support holds (often on heavy volume marking the end of selling), the second bottom forms and the trend reverses upward.

**Measure rule:** Compute the height from the highest high between the two bottoms down to the lower of the two bottom lows. Add that difference to the highest high. The result is the minimum price target.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: break-even failure rate 5%, average rise 35%, change after trend ends –33%, throwbacks 64%, meets price target 66%.
- Bear market: break-even failure rate 7%, average rise 24%, change after trend ends –32%, throwbacks 61%, meets price target 48%.
- Volume trend: downward.
- Busted pattern: –32% bull, –35% bear (few samples).

**Trading tactics:**
- Entry: Buy on a close above the confirmation line (breakout). Buying before breakout fails about 64% of the time.
- Stop: Place just below the right bottom low or below the throwback shelf.
- Trade with the trend: Best results in a bull market.
- Throwback: Occurs nearly two-thirds of the time; add to or initiate a position once price begins rebounding from the throwback.
- For-Best-Performance: Select bull market patterns (lowest failure rates); avoid short-and-wide patterns; choose tall patterns; prefer a lower right bottom; look for a falling volume trend, dome-shaped volume, heavy breakout volume, and volume heavier on the left bottom; check that other stocks in the same industry are bottoming; avoid overhead resistance because throwbacks hurt performance.

## Double Bottoms, Adam & Eve (AEDB)

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal; forms at the end of a downward price trend (or as the corrective phase of a measured move up). All act as reversals of the downtrend by definition.

**Identification:**
- Two bottoms that look different from each other: the left bottom (Adam) is narrow, V-shaped, and often has a one- or two-day downward spike; the right bottom (Eve) is wide, rounded, with many short spikes.
- Price trends downward into the pattern and should not drift below the left bottom.
- Rise between bottoms: at least 10%; taller patterns perform better.
- Bottom-to-bottom price variation should be small; a lower right bottom is preferred.
- Bottom separation: best 2–6 weeks; performance deteriorates past 8 weeks.
- Price must close above the confirmation point without first falling below the right bottom low; this close is the breakout.
- Volume usually higher on the left bottom than the right; falling volume trend is preferred.

**Psychology:** A double bottom is a retest of the low. Sellers exhaust themselves at the first narrow Adam spike, buyers step in, a rebound occurs, and then price retests the low — this time holding as a wider, rounded Eve bottom — before reversing upward.

**Measure rule:** Compute the height from the highest high between the two bottoms down to the lowest low in the pattern. Add that difference to the highest high. The result is the minimum price target.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: break-even failure rate 5%, average rise 37%, change after trend ends –33%, throwbacks 59%, meets price target 66%.
- Bear market: break-even failure rate 4%, average rise 33%, change after trend ends –35%, throwbacks 54%, meets price target 56%.
- Volume trend: downward.
- Busted pattern: –28% bull, –54% bear (few samples).

**Trading tactics:**
- Entry: Buy on a close above the confirmation line. Buying before breakout fails about 64% of the time.
- Stop: Place just below the right bottom low or below the throwback shelf.
- Trade with the trend: Slight edge in a bull market; avoid trading bullish patterns against the market.
- Throwback: Occurs about half the time; initiate or add once price begins rebounding.
- For-Best-Performance: Select bull market patterns; choose patterns with breakouts near the yearly high; pick tall patterns; favor a falling volume trend from bottom to bottom; prefer volume heavier on the left bottom; check other stocks in the same industry are bottoming; avoid overhead resistance because throwbacks hurt performance; in a bear market watch for momentum weakness a month after breakout.

## Double Bottoms, Eve & Adam (EADB)

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal; forms at the end of a downward price trend (or as the corrective phase of a measured move up). All act as reversals of the downtrend by definition.

**Identification:**
- Two bottoms that look different from each other: the left bottom (Eve) is wide and rounded; the right bottom (Adam) is narrow, V-shaped, often with one or two long downward spikes.
- Price trends downward into the pattern and should not drift below the left bottom; leading downtrend averages about 4 months.
- Rise between bottoms: at least 10%; taller patterns perform better.
- Bottom-to-bottom price variation should be small; best performance with 0%–4% variation; a lower left bottom is preferred.
- Bottom separation: best 2–7 weeks; performance deteriorates past 7 weeks.
- Price must close above the confirmation point without first falling below the right bottom low; this close is the breakout.
- Volume can be heavier on either bottom; the rarest of the four double-bottom variants.

**Psychology:** A double bottom is a retest of the low. After the wide, rounded Eve bottom forms and buying demand balances selling pressure, prices rebound; sellers reassert and price retests, forming a sharper, narrower Adam spike, which then fails as buyers overwhelm supply and price reverses upward.

**Measure rule:** Compute the height from the highest high between the two bottoms down to the lowest bottom low. Add that difference to the highest high. The result is the minimum price target.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: break-even failure rate 4%, average rise 35%, change after trend ends –31%, throwbacks 57%, meets price target 66%.
- Bear market: break-even failure rate 8%, average rise 23%, change after trend ends –36%, throwbacks 56%, meets price target 47%.
- Volume trend: downward.
- Busted pattern: –31% bull, –29% bear (few samples).

**Trading tactics:**
- Entry: Buy on a close above the confirmation line. Buying before breakout fails about 64% of the time.
- Stop: Place just below the right bottom low or below the throwback shelf.
- Trade with the trend: Strongly prefer a bull market; bear market patterns perform poorly.
- Throwback: Occurs about 56% of the time; add to or initiate a position once price begins rebounding.
- For-Best-Performance: Select bull market patterns (lowest failure rates); choose patterns with breakouts near the yearly low; pick patterns narrower than the median length; prefer a lower left bottom; favor a falling volume trend and dome-shaped volume; light breakout volume works best; choose volume heavier on the left bottom; check other stocks in the same industry are bottoming; avoid overhead resistance because throwbacks hurt performance in a bull market; watch for price weakness a month after breakout in a bear market.

## Double Bottoms, Eve & Eve (EEDB)

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal; forms at the end of a downward price trend. All act as reversals of the downtrend by definition. The "classic" double bottom shape.

**Identification:**
- Two wide, rounded, similar-looking bottoms (Eve on both sides). Both may show short price spikes, but the surrounding turn is rounded rather than V-shaped.
- Price trends downward into the pattern and should not drift below the left bottom.
- Rise between bottoms: at least 10%, but exceptions allowed.
- Bottom-to-bottom price variation should be small; best performance with 0%–6% variation; a lower right bottom is preferred.
- Bottom separation: best 2–7 weeks; performance deteriorates past 7 weeks.
- Price must close above the confirmation point without first falling below the right bottom low; this close is the breakout.
- Volume usually higher on the left bottom than the right, with a downward overall volume trend; however, patterns with heavier volume on the right bottom do well.

**Psychology:** Prices fall until traders view the stock as a bargain and buy, with high volume marking the bottom. The first Eve bottom forms as buying and selling balance over a gentle, rounded turn. Prices rise, then sellers take profits and the stock falls back. The second Eve bottom forms as buyers re-enter. When price approaches the prior high, profit-taking stalls it, but demand overwhelms supply and the breakout above the high between the bottoms triggers a surge in buying — the pattern is confirmed.

**Measure rule:** Compute the height from the highest high between the two bottoms down to the lowest bottom low. Add that difference to the highest high. The result is the minimum price target.

**Key numbers:**
- Bull market: break-even failure rate 4%, average rise 40%, change after trend ends –31%, throwbacks 55%, meets price target 67%.
- Bear market: break-even failure rate 7%, average rise 24%, change after trend ends –34%, throwbacks 46%, meets price target 54%.
- Volume trend: downward.
- Busted pattern: –31% bull, –30% bear (few samples).
- Highest average rise of the four double-bottom variants in a bull market.

**Trading tactics:**
- Entry: Buy on a close above the confirmation line. Buying before breakout fails about 64% of the time. Exception: if a flat shelf forms near the right bottom, buy on a close above the shelf with a stop below it.
- Stop: Place just below the right bottom low or below the throwback shelf.
- Trade with the trend: Best results in a bull market; bear market rises are on par with other chart pattern types.
- Throwback: Occurs about half the time; throwbacks hurt performance, so add or initiate only after price begins rebounding.
- For-Best-Performance: Trade in a bull market for lower failure rates; select patterns with breakouts near the yearly low; watch for the rise to stall 5–6 weeks after breakout in a bear market; choose narrow patterns; prefer a lower right bottom; select patterns with volume heavier on the right bottom than the left; check other stocks in the same industry are bottoming; avoid overhead resistance because throwbacks hurt performance.
