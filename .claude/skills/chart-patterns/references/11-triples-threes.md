# Triple Tops/Bottoms & Three Peaks/Valleys

## Three Falling Peaks (3FP)

**Type:** Short-term bearish reversal; bearish; appears most often at the end of an uptrend (reversal), occasionally as a continuation of a downtrend.

**Identification:** Three peaks, each with a high lower than the prior peak, proportional in size and shape. Peaks are not tied; they stair-step downward from the highest high where the uptrend ends. Volume trend is downward; U-shaped volume outperforms dome-shaped. The pattern confirms when price closes below the lowest low in the formation (or below an up-sloping trend line connecting the two valley lows). If the lowest low lies between peaks 1 and 2, the valley between peaks 2 and 3 may be used as an earlier confirmation price.

**Psychology:** Successive lower highs show buyers losing conviction at each push up, while sellers defend lower prices. By the third lower peak, supply overwhelms demand and the prior uptrend reverses.

**Measure rule:** Compute formation height as the highest high minus the lowest low. Subtract that height from the lowest low to obtain the price target.

**Key numbers:**
- Average decline: 17% bull market, 24% bear market
- Break-even failure rate: 12% bull, 4% bear
- Pullbacks: 59% bull, 62% bear (pullbacks hurt performance)
- Percentage meeting price target: 33% bull, 32% bear
- Change after trend ends: 56% bull, 52% bear
- Busted-pattern performance (drop <5% then reverse): 58% bull, 53% bear
- Decline measured from top of pattern averages 34% bull, 44% bear

**Trading tactics:** Enter short on a close below the lowest low (or trend-line break). For early entry, when the second valley is higher than the first, use the second (higher) valley as the breakout price. A close below an up-sloping trend line connecting the two valleys can also signal the breakout. Stop placement: just above the lowest peak (a nearby minor high). For best performance: trade in a bear market (largest decline, lowest failure rate), trade busted patterns, select narrow patterns, pick U-shaped volume, get in quickly because prices often bottom within 2–3 weeks.

## Three Rising Valleys (3RV)

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal; bullish; appears most often in an uptrend, sometimes as a reversal of a downtrend.

**Identification:** Three minor lows, each higher than the prior one, similar in shape and proportion. Volume trend is downward overall; U-shaped volume and rising volume trends perform best. The pattern confirms when price closes above the highest high in the formation. If the highest high occurs between the first two valleys, use the high between valleys 2 and 3 as an earlier confirmation price.

**Psychology:** Rising troughs show buyers stepping in at progressively higher prices, signaling accumulation. The repeated defense of higher lows eventually overwhelms supply and launches an upside breakout.

**Measure rule:** Compute formation height as the highest high minus the lowest low. Add that height to the breakout price (highest high) to obtain the price target.

**Key numbers:**
- Average rise: 41% bull market, 22% bear market
- Break-even failure rate: 5% bull, 9% bear
- Throwbacks: 60% bull, 65% bear (throwbacks hurt performance in a bull market)
- Percentage meeting price target: 58% bull, 42% bear
- Change after trend ends: −33% bull, −33% bear
- Busted-pattern performance (rise <5% then drop): −34% bull, −30% bear
- Days to ultimate high: 125 bull, 94 bear

**Trading tactics:** Buy on a close above the highest high (or trend-line break). Early entry: if the highest high sits between the first two valleys, use the last peak (between valleys 2 and 3) as the breakout price. A close above a down-sloping trend line drawn between the two peaks also confirms the pattern. Trade reversals of a downtrend in a bull market for best results. For best performance: select in a bull market (lower failure rates, higher average rise), pick breakouts near the yearly high, choose tall patterns, select rising volume trend with U-shape, prefer light breakout volume, expect a throwback on roughly two of three trades.

## Triple Bottoms

**Type:** Short-term bullish reversal; bullish; usually forms at the end of a downtrend, sometimes as a continuation/consolidation of an uptrend.

**Identification:** Three large, distinct, well-separated minor lows at approximately the same price. The center bottom should not be significantly below the other two (otherwise suspect a head-and-shoulders bottom). Rises between bottoms often appear rounded; bottoms are often sharp V-shaped spikes. Volume trend is generally downward, with the first bottom usually showing the highest volume. Pattern confirms when price closes above the highest high in the formation (the confirmation point). Patterns are often large enough to be visible on weekly charts.

**Psychology:** The same support level holds three times, signalling a powerful floor where buyers consistently absorb supply. Once overhead supply is cleared, the pattern resolves upward.

**Measure rule:** Compute formation height as the highest high minus the lowest low. Add that height to the highest high (the confirmation point) to obtain the expected minimum price move.

**Key numbers:**
- Average rise: 37% bull market, 23% bear market
- Break-even failure rate: 4% bull, 8% bear
- Throwbacks: 64% bull, 61% bear (throwbacks hurt performance)
- Percentage meeting price target: 64% bull, 57% bear
- Change after trend ends: −33% bull, −36% bear
- Busted-pattern performance (rise <5% then drop): −29% bull, −38% bear
- Rises over 45%: 32% bull, 17% bear
- Days to ultimate high: 165 bull, 80 bear

**Trading tactics:** Always wait for a close above the highest high before buying. If a down-sloping trend line connects the two intervening highs, buy on a close above that line for an earlier entry. Because throwbacks occur in nearly two-thirds of trades, consider waiting for the throwback and the resumption upward before initiating or adding. Stop placement: about 0.10 below the lowest low (or just below a nearer support zone); raise the stop as price rises. For best performance: trade in a bull market (lower failure rates, higher rises), trade busted patterns, expect a short and steep rise in a bear market, select short and narrow patterns, prefer dome-shaped volume in a bull market and U-shaped in a bear market, in a bull market prefer a third valley whose price is higher than the middle valley.

## Triple Tops

**Type:** Short-term bearish reversal; bearish; usually forms at the top of an uptrend, occasionally as a continuation of a downtrend.

**Identification:** Three large, distinct, well-separated minor highs at approximately the same price. The center top should not tower above the other two (otherwise suspect a head-and-shoulders top). Tops are often sharp spikes with rounded valleys. Volume trend is generally downward and often flat, with the first peak usually showing the highest volume. Pattern confirms when price closes below the lowest low in the formation (the confirmation point). An up-sloping trend line drawn across the two valley lows can also serve as confirmation and may allow an earlier entry. Patterns are often large enough to be visible on weekly charts.

**Psychology:** The same resistance level caps price three times, showing overwhelming supply. Each failed rally exhausts buyers, and once support gives way, the pattern resolves downward.

**Measure rule:** Compute formation height as the highest high minus the lowest low. Subtract that height from the lowest low to obtain the expected minimum price move.

**Key numbers:**
- Average decline: 19% bull market, 24% bear market
- Break-even failure rate: 10% bull, 5% bear
- Pullbacks: 61% bull, 64% bear (pullbacks hurt performance)
- Percentage meeting price target: 40% bull, 51% bear
- Change after trend ends: 53% bull, 46% bear
- Busted-pattern performance (drop <5% then rise): 63% bull, 33% bear
- Declines over 45%: 4% bull, 7% bear
- Days to ultimate low: 60 bull, 42 bear

**Trading tactics:** Sell or short only after a close below the confirmation point (or below an up-sloping trend line connecting the two valley lows for an earlier entry). Because pullbacks occur in over 60% of patterns, traders who miss the original breakout can add on the pullback once price resumes downward. Stop placement for shorts: about 0.10 above the nearest high (the resistance zone). For best performance: short in a bear market (lowest failure rate, steepest decline), trade busted patterns in a bull market, look for underlying support to size the decline, prefer breakout-day gaps (they help performance), select tall or narrow patterns and avoid short and wide ones, choose U-shaped volume and avoid dome-shaped volume, prefer last-peak volume below middle-peak volume, in a bull market prefer the last top at the same price as the middle top, and in a bear market prefer the last top higher than the middle top.
