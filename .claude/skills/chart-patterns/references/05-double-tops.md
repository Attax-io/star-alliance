# Double Tops (Adam & Eve variants)

## Adam & Adam Double Top (AADT)

**Type:** Bearish reversal; forms in an uptrend (average prior trend 113 days bull / 70 days bear).

**Identification:** Two peaks that look similar: each is narrow, pointed, and shaped like an inverted V (a hypodermic-needle spike, usually a one- or two-day price spike). Best performance when peak-to-peak price variation is 0%–6% and tops sit 5–7 weeks apart. A dip between the peaks separates them — do not select twin spikes from the same congestion. Volume is usually higher on the left peak and trends downward peak-to-peak; U-shaped volume is most common. The twin-peak pattern becomes a true AADT only when price closes below the confirmation line (the lowest low between the two peaks). Until confirmation, 65% of twin-peak patterns continue rising.

**Psychology:** The first spike exhausts momentum buyers in a single sharp push. A second failed attempt at the same price leaves a narrow, fragile top. When the valley floor gives way, both the bulls' last stand and the support beneath it are broken.

**Measure rule:** Take the pattern height (highest high minus the lowest low between the two peaks), divide by 2, and subtract that value from the lowest low to get the target. (Half-height is used, not full height.) Ignore the result if it goes negative.

**Key numbers:** Average decline 19% in both bull and bear markets. Break-even failure rate 8% bull, 11% bear (5% failure). Pullbacks 61% bull, 48% bear. Days to ultimate low 51 bull, 32 bear. Formation end to breakout about 28–29 days. Busted-pattern rebound 40% bull, 37% bear. Change after trend ends 54% bull, 47% bear. Declines over 45% occur 5% of the time in both markets. Tall-and-narrow patterns perform best (–25% bull, –19% bear); avoid short-and-wide patterns. Heavy breakout volume outperforms (–20% in both). Patterns with a lower left top outperform.

**Trading tactics:** Enter on a close below the confirmation line; a stop a dime or so above the most recent minor high is a common approach. If shorting pre-confirmation, accept that 65% of the time price will not reach the confirmation line. Trade in a bear market for steeper, more frequent declines. Watch the same industry group — if peers are topping or falling, the move is more likely to follow through. For best performance: trade in a bear market, select tall-and-narrow patterns with a lower left top, look for heavy breakout volume, and use breakouts near the yearly low in bear markets or near the yearly high in bull markets. Use a stop to limit loss to the 5% failure threshold.

## Adam & Eve Double Top (AEDT)

**Type:** Bearish reversal; forms in an uptrend (average prior rise ~128 days, ~4 months).

**Identification:** First peak is an Adam top (narrow inverted-V spike). Second peak is an Eve top (wide, rounded, often made of several short spikes). The two peaks should look distinctly different. Valley between tops usually 10%–20% deep (larger dips perform better). Peak-to-peak price variation usually 0%–3%. Most peaks sit 2–7 weeks apart. Volume is usually heavier on the left peak. Confirmation requires a close below the lowest low between the two peaks; otherwise the pattern is not a valid double top.

**Psychology:** A measured move up ends with a sharp Adam spike as the move completes. Then the stock consolidates and forms a slower, rounded Eve top as buyers return at the same level. When that second, more deliberate attempt also fails, the broader support gives way and the reversal is confirmed.

**Measure rule:** Half the formation height (highest high minus the lowest low between the peaks), subtracted from the lowest low, gives the target.

**Key numbers:** Average decline 18% bull, 22% bear. Break-even failure rate 14% bull, 7% bear. Pullbacks 59% bull, 58% bear (pullbacks hurt performance). Days to ultimate low 49 bull, 45 bear. Formation end to breakout 41 days bull, 34 days bear. Busted-pattern rebound 43% bull, 35% bear. Change after trend ends 50% bull, 43% bear. Declines over 45% occur 4% bull, 8% bear. Tall patterns outperform short; tall-and-narrow is the best combination (–25% in both markets). Patterns with a lower right top outperform (–18% bull, –25% bear). U-shaped volume performs best (–19% bull, –23% bear). Bear-market breakouts often have larger gaps ($2.41 vs $0.74).

**Trading tactics:** Wait for the close below the confirmation line before selling or shorting. Place a stop just above the nearest prior minor high; ratchet the stop down as price makes new lows. Trade in a bear market for best performance. In a bull market, look for nearby support and consider whether a small decline is tolerable rather than selling into a strong tape. For best performance: trade in a bear market, choose tall-and-narrow patterns with a lower right top, prefer U-shaped volume, and avoid patterns with underlying support that will trigger a pullback. Watch for a counter-trend bounce about one month after the breakout (bull) or around days 14–21 (bear) and consider covering then.

## Eve & Adam Double Top (EADT)

**Type:** Bearish reversal; forms in an uptrend (average prior trend ~84 days, ~3 months).

**Identification:** First peak is an Eve top (wide, rounded). Second peak is an Adam top (narrow inverted-V spike). The two peaks must look different. Valley between tops 10%–25% deep. Peak-to-peak price variation usually 0%–3%, but a larger-than-median variation actually performs better. Most peaks 2–6 weeks apart; performance deteriorates if wider than 5 weeks. This is the only Adam/Eve variant where volume is typically heavier on the right (Adam) top. Confirmation requires a close below the lowest low between the two peaks.

**Psychology:** The rounded Eve top reflects a slow build-up of buying near overhead resistance, while the sharp Adam spike on the right is a final blow-off as late buyers pile in. That single-day exhaustion leaves the stock unsupported; the break of the valley floor confirms the buyers have been overwhelmed.

**Measure rule:** Half the formation height (highest high minus the lowest low between the peaks), subtracted from the lowest low, gives the target.

**Key numbers:** Average decline 15% bull, 24% bear. Break-even failure rate 13% bull, 5% bear. Pullbacks 64% bull, 54% bear (pullbacks hurt performance). Days to ultimate low 43 bull, 40 bear. Formation end to breakout 34 days bull, 29 days bear. Busted-pattern rebound 63% bull, 55% bear. Change after trend ends 54% bull, 51% bear. Declines over 45% occur 4% bull, 6% bear. Volume trend is unusual: most EADTs trend upward. Tall and narrow patterns perform best (–19% bull, –29% bear). Patterns with a large peak-to-peak price variation outperform. Light breakout volume outperforms (counter to most patterns).

**Trading tactics:** Wait for the close below the confirmation line; pre-confirmation shorts face a 65% chance price never reaches confirmation. Place a stop above the most recent minor high and ratchet it lower with each new low. Trade in a bear market for best performance — bear-market failure rates are roughly a third of bull-market rates. For best performance: trade in a bear market, choose tall or narrow patterns, select those with a large peak-to-peak price variation, prefer light breakout volume, and avoid patterns with nearby support that will trigger a pullback. Be ready to cover shorts about a month after the breakout, when counter-trend bounces tend to start.

## Eve & Eve Double Top (EEDT)

**Type:** Bearish reversal; forms in an uptrend. The "classic" double top — both peaks wide and rounded, looking the same.

**Identification:** Two peaks that look similar — both wide and rounded (Eve), often composed of several short spikes rather than a single needle. Peak-to-peak price variation usually 0%–3%. Most peaks 2–6 weeks apart. Volume is usually heavier on the left peak than the right (about 60% of the time); the U-shape is the most common volume pattern. Confirmation requires a close below the lowest low between the two peaks. Without confirmation, 65% of twin-peak patterns climb away instead of confirming.

**Psychology:** Two successive attempts to push through overhead resistance both fail. The wide, rounded tops reflect widespread distribution — heavy selling into each rally — and the repeated rejection of the same price level. When the valley floor finally gives way, supply has clearly overwhelmed demand.

**Measure rule:** Half the formation height (highest high minus the lowest low between the peaks), subtracted from the lowest low, gives the target.

**Key numbers:** Average decline 18% bull, 25% bear. Break-even failure rate 11% bull, 2% bear. Pullbacks 62% bull, 51% bear (pullbacks hurt performance). Days to ultimate low 44 bull, 39 bear. Formation end to breakout 35 days bull, 34 days bear. Busted-pattern rebound 70% bull, 29% bear (note small bear-market sample). Change after trend ends 63% bull, 45% bear — one of the largest rebounds in any bearish pattern. Declines over 45% occur 2% bull, 9% bear. Volume trend downward in both markets. Tall-and-narrow patterns perform best (–20% bull, –28% bear); short-and-wide are the worst. Heavy left-top volume outperforms. U-shaped volume performs best (–19% bull, –26% bear). In a bull market, small peak-to-peak price variation outperforms; in a bear market, results are similar regardless of variation. Many patterns (25%–29%) reach the ultimate low in the first week; expect a turn around day 28 (bull) or day 35 (bear).

**Trading tactics:** Wait for the close below the confirmation line; pre-confirmation, price will not drop to confirmation 65% of the time. After the breakout, a stop just above the most recent minor high is standard. Short in a bear market — failure rates are roughly half those in a bull market. Check the same industry group; weakness in peers confirms the move. For best performance: trade in a bear market, select tall-and-narrow patterns, prefer those with heavy left-top volume and U-shaped volume, and consider waiting for the trend change at the bottom to buy (especially in a bull market, where the post-bottom rise averages 63%). Avoid patterns sitting on nearby support that will trigger a pullback.
