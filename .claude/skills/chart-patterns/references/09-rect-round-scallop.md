# Rectangles, Rounding Turns & Scallops

## Rectangle Bottoms
**Type:** Continuation (downward breakout) or short-term bullish reversal (upward breakout); appears after a downward price trend.
**Identification:** Prices oscillate between two horizontal (or nearly so) trend lines before breaking out. At least two touches of each trend line required (four total); touches need not alternate. Volume usually recedes regardless of breakout direction. Breakout is a close outside the bounding trend line.
**Psychology:** A battle between buyers and sellers pinned between support and resistance; one side eventually exhausts and price escapes the congestion zone.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the lower trend line value from the upper trend line to get the formation height. Add the height to the upper trend line for an upward breakout target; subtract the height from the lower trend line for a downward breakout target.
**Key numbers:** Bull market, upward breakout: 46% average rise, 10% break-even failure, 53% throwback, 85% meet target. Bull market, downward breakout: 14% average decline, 16% break-even failure, 69% pullback. Bear market, upward breakout: 24% average rise, 11% break-even failure, 60% throwback, 64% meet target. Bear market, downward breakout: 25% average decline, 4% break-even failure, 53% pullback, 66% meet target. Tall and wide patterns perform best. Rising or random volume trend outperforms; partial declines predict upward breakout 81% of the time, partial rises predict downward 83%.
**Trading tactics:** Wait for the breakout and trade in the breakout direction. Place the stop just beyond the opposite trend line. Use throwbacks/pullbacks to add to the position once price resumes the breakout direction. For best performance: trade with the prevailing market trend; select tall, wide rectangles; prefer patterns without a pre-formation rise; look for rising volume and heavy breakout volume.

## Rectangle Tops
**Type:** Continuation (upward breakout) or bearish reversal (downward breakout); appears after an upward price trend.
**Identification:** Prices oscillate between two horizontal trend lines. At least two touches of each trend line; touches need not alternate. Rising price trend leading in. Volume usually recedes until the breakout.
**Psychology:** Owners have identified a sell price and dump shares when reached; buyers place buy orders at perceived fair value. When one side runs out of orders, price pierces a boundary.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the lower trend line value from the upper trend line to get the formation height. Add the height to the upper trend line for an upward breakout target; subtract it from the lower trend line for a downward breakout target.
**Key numbers:** Bull market, upward breakout: 39% average rise, 9% break-even failure, 64% throwback, 80% meet target. Bull market, downward breakout: 17% average decline, 11% break-even failure, 58% pullback, 63% meet target. Bear market, upward breakout: 20% average rise, 16% break-even failure, 71% throwback, 60% meet target. Bear market, downward breakout: 21% average decline, 9% break-even failure, 65% pullback, 63% meet target. Tall or wide patterns perform better. Falling volume trend helps upward breakouts; heavy breakout volume helps. Partial declines predict upward breakout 89% of the time; partial rises predict downward 61% of the time. Pre-formation drop improves performance.
**Trading tactics:** Wait for the breakout close outside the trend line, then trade with the breakout direction. Stop just beyond the opposite trend line. Use throwbacks and pullbacks to add or enter with reduced risk. For best performance: trade with the market trend; select tall or wide patterns; prefer pre-formation drops; favor rectangles with breakouts near the yearly low; avoid breakout day gaps.

## Rounding Bottoms
**Type:** Bullish consolidation; acts as continuation more often than reversal, especially in uptrends.
**Identification:** Saucer- or bowl-shaped price pattern on the daily or weekly scale. Often long (weeks to years). The rounded bottom need not be smooth — minor irregularities are common. A handle frequently forms after the right rim. Volume often dome-shaped (54%) or U-shaped (46%).
**Psychology:** Selling pressure initially dominates, then forces balance, then buying demand gradually overwhelms supply as the climb resumes.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low in the saucer from the right saucer lip; add the difference to the right saucer lip to get the target price.
**Key numbers:** Bull market: 43% average rise, 5% break-even failure, 40% throwback, 57% meet target. Bear market: 31% average rise, 5% break-even failure, 43% throwback, 53% meet target. Tall or wide patterns perform better. Rising volume trend and U-shape slightly outperform. Throwbacks hurt performance (33% with vs. 50% without in bull market).
**Trading tactics:** Wait for price to close above the left or right saucer lip (whichever is lower) before buying. Watch for a handle to form after the right rim. For best performance: trade in a bull market; prefer continuations over reversals in a bull market; select tall or wide patterns; pick breakouts near the yearly high; avoid throwbacks; favor higher left rim; choose rising volume trend and U-shaped volume with heavy breakout volume.

## Rounding Tops
**Type:** Bullish continuation (upward breakout, 53% of the time) or bearish reversal (downward breakout); appears in an upward price trend.
**Identification:** Smooth, rounded half-moon or inverted-U shape. Start and end prices should be close (average difference 3%, with end higher 52% of the time). Volume often lowest in the center, higher at the ends. Upward breakout = close above the pattern's highest high; downward breakout = close below the right rim.
**Psychology:** Initial buying enthusiasm wanes as supply catches up; smart money begins distributing; once trend change is recognized, broader selling intensifies.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the right rim low from the highest high in the formation to get the height. Add the height to the highest high for an upward breakout target; subtract the height from the right rim low for a downward breakout target.
**Key numbers:** Bull market, upward breakout: 37% average rise, 9% break-even failure, 53% throwback, 61% meet target. Bull market, downward breakout: 19% average decline, 12% break-even failure, 48% pullback, 24% meet target. Bear market, upward breakout: 19% average rise, 16% break-even failure, 52% throwback, 35% meet target. Bear market, downward breakout: 23% average decline, 9% break-even failure, 57% pullback, 15% meet target. Tall or tall-and-narrow patterns perform best. Falling volume trend, U-shaped volume, and heavy breakout volume help. Busted patterns perform well.
**Trading tactics:** Buy or short on a close outside the pattern boundary. Optionally enter on a 30% retrace of the formation height above the right dome low for more risk but better entry. Use the right rim low as support reference. For best performance: trade with the market trend; prefer continuations; select tall or tall-and-narrow patterns; favor falling volume, U-shape, and heavy breakout volume; pick patterns with a higher right rim; choose breakouts with gaps.

## Scallops, Ascending
**Type:** Bullish continuation (upward breakout) or bearish reversal (downward breakout); appears in a rising price trend.
**Identification:** J shape with the right peak above the left peak and a rounded recession between them. Volume often U-shaped. In a series of scallops, formations tend to get narrower and shorter as the trend matures. Breakout is a close above the highest high (upward) or below the lowest low (downward).
**Psychology:** Trend consolidation; each scallop is a brief rest before the climb resumes. A series of narrowing, shortening scallops signals trend exhaustion.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low in the bowl from the right-side high to get the formation height. Add the height to the right-side high for an upward breakout target; subtract it from the lowest low for a downward breakout target.
**Key numbers:** Bull market, upward breakout: 31% average rise, 10% break-even failure, 58% throwback, 58% meet target. Bull market, downward breakout: 14% average decline, 27% break-even failure, 56% pullback, 25% meet target. Bear market, upward breakout: 19% average rise, 16% break-even failure, 42% throwback, 39% meet target. Bear market, downward breakout: 19% average decline, 14% break-even failure, 57% pullback, 30% meet target. Tall patterns outperform. Rising volume trend, U-shape (upward breakouts) or dome shape (downward breakouts), and heavy breakout volume help.
**Trading tactics:** Wait for breakout. Place stop about $0.15 below the formation low. Use handle (decline after right crest) to add or enter. For best performance: trade with the market trend; prefer reversals (except bear market down breakouts); select tall patterns; favor scallops near the yearly low; pick rising volume and breakout-day gaps.

## Scallops, Ascending and Inverted
**Type:** Bullish continuation; appears in an upward price trend (or at a bullish turning point of a downtrend).
**Identification:** Looks like a backward, upside-down J or the right half of an open umbrella. Smooth top connecting the highs (allow minor spikes). Average top retrace is about 50% of the prior up move. Price must not drop below the pattern start. Confirmation requires a close above the pattern's highest high.
**Psychology:** Often the corrective phase of a measured move down in a downtrend, or a brief pause within an established uptrend before the climb resumes.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the pattern low from the pattern high to get the formation height; add the height to the pattern high to get the target price.
**Key numbers:** Bull market: 43% average rise, 4% break-even failure, 61% throwback, 61% meet target. Bear market: 26% average rise, 7% break-even failure, 65% throwback, 55% meet target. Tall patterns, and tall-and-narrow in a bull market, perform best. In a series, scallops get narrower and shorter. Reversals outperform continuations. Heavy breakout volume helps in a bull market.
**Trading tactics:** Search for nearby support and resistance before entry. Wait for confirmation (close above pattern high). For best performance: trade in a bull market; select tall or tall-and-narrow patterns; prefer breakouts near the yearly low; avoid throwbacks; use heavy breakout volume; recognize that narrow or short scallops in a series may signal a coming trend change.

## Scallops, Descending
**Type:** Bearish continuation (downward breakout) or bullish reversal (upward breakout); appears in a downward price trend 64% of the time.
**Identification:** Reverse-J shape with the left peak higher than the right. Bowl should be a smooth, rounded turn. Start and end should be far apart in price; the right peak usually retraces about 60% of the distance to the left peak. Volume often dome-shaped (70%). Upward breakout = close above the right peak; downward breakout = close below the bowl low.
**Psychology:** A bounce within a declining trend; the failure of the bounce (downward breakout) confirms the prevailing downtrend.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the lowest low from the highest high to get the formation height. Add the height to the right scallop peak for an upward breakout target; subtract the height from the lowest low for a downward breakout target.
**Key numbers:** Bull market, upward breakout: 22% average rise, 22% break-even failure, 62% throwback, 35% meet target. Bull market, downward breakout: 17% average decline, 15% break-even failure, 55% pullback, 30% meet target. Bear market, upward breakout: 20% average rise, 20% break-even failure, 58% throwback, 33% meet target. Bear market, downward breakout: 23% average decline, 8% break-even failure, 52% pullback, 32% meet target. Tall or wide patterns perform better. Rising volume trend and dome-shaped volume help upward breakouts. Gaps improve performance.
**Trading tactics:** Wait for breakout. For downward breakouts, place stop slightly above the right lip high; for upward breakouts, place stop below the formation low. Trade with the market trend, especially shorting in a bear market. For best performance: trade downward breakouts in a bear market; select tall or wide patterns; favor breakouts near the yearly low (upward) or middle/high (downward); pick rising volume, dome shape (upward), and heavy breakout volume; use breakout day gaps.

## Scallops, Descending and Inverted
**Type:** Bearish continuation (downward breakout); appears in a downward price trend.
**Identification:** Upside-down J shape. The start (point A) is higher in price than the end (point C). The inverted bowl portion averages about 55% of the length of the following decline. Confirmation requires price to close below the lowest low without first exceeding the pattern's highest high.
**Psychology:** A failed bounce within a downtrend; once price breaks the formation low, the downtrend resumes.
**Measure rule:** Subtract the formation low from the formation high to get the height; subtract the height from the formation low to get the target price.
**Key numbers:** Bull market: 18% average decline, 10% break-even failure, 58% pullback, 38% meet target. Bear market: 23% average decline, 5% break-even failure, 50% pullback, 27% meet target. Tall-and-wide patterns perform best. Falling volume trend and U-shaped volume (bear market) help. In a bull market, scallops in a series get narrower and shorter; in a bear market, they get wider and taller. Pullbacks hurt performance.
**Trading tactics:** Short on a close below the formation low. Cover if price retraces to the start price level; close the position immediately if price exceeds the pattern high. Search for nearby support and resistance before trading. For best performance: trade in a bear market (faster, larger declines); select tall-and-wide patterns; favor breakouts near the yearly low (bear market); prefer falling volume trend, U-shaped volume (bear market), and heavy breakout volume; expect weakness about a month into the trade.
