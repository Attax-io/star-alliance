# Stars — Morning, Evening, Doji Stars, Shooting Star

## Star (General)

- **Type:** Reversal pattern that can be bullish or bearish depending on prior trend context · appears at tops (after uptrend) or bottoms (after downtrend) · minimum two candle lines (large real body + small real body star).
- **Structure:** A small real body that gaps away from the large real body preceding it. The star's real body must not overlap the prior real body. The colour of the star (white or black) is unimportant. When a star appears during a downtrend it is sometimes labelled a "rain drop." If the star is a doji instead of a small real body, it becomes a doji star.
- **Recognition:** Identify a large real body candle (the trend candle), then identify a following candle with a small real body whose real body does not overlap the previous real body. Gapping is the defining element.
- **Psychology:** In a strong uptrend, bulls are in charge. The emergence of a star after a long white candlestick signals a shift from buyers being in control to a deadlock between buying and selling forces. This deadlock may stem from a diminution in buying force or an increase in selling force. In a downtrend, the mirror effect occurs: bears were in command, but the star signals an environment in which bulls and bears are more in equilibrium and the downward energy has cooled.
- **Signal & confirmation:** The star, especially the doji star, is a warning that the prior trend may be ending. The star's small real body represents a stalemate in the tug of war between bulls and bears, telling us the prior trend power has dissipated and the market is vulnerable to a setback.
- **Nison's notes:** The star is part of four reversal patterns: the evening star, the morning star, the doji star, and the shooting star. In any of these star patterns the real body of the star can be white or black.

## Doji Star (General)

- **Type:** Reversal · can be top or bottom depending on trend context · two-line minimum.
- **Structure:** A doji whose real body (opening and closing price) gaps away from the prior candle's real body. In a rising market, the doji gaps above the prior real body; in a falling market, the doji gaps under the prior real body.
- **Recognition:** Locate a doji whose real body is clearly separated (by a gap) from the preceding real body.
- **Psychology:** The doji reflects complete indecision after a directional move — a potent warning that the prior trend is apt to change.
- **Signal & confirmation:** The session after the doji should confirm the trend reversal.
- **Nison's notes:** A pattern is still called a star even when the shadow of the doji star overlaps the prior day's black real body. The open/close gap on the real body is the key requirement; the shadow may overlap.

## Morning Star

- **Type:** Bullish · bottom reversal · three candle lines.
- **Structure:**
  - Line 1: Tall, black real body (downtrend candle).
  - Line 2: Small real body that gaps lower than Line 1 (these two lines together constitute the basic star pattern). Colour is not important.
  - Line 3: White real body that moves well within the first period's black real body.
  - An ideal pattern has a gap before the middle line's real body AND a gap after the middle line's real body.
- **Recognition:** In a downtrend, identify a tall black real body, followed by a small real body that gaps lower, followed by a white real body that closes substantially up into the first candle's black real body.
- **Psychology:** The market is in a downtrend and bears are in command. Then a small real body appears, meaning sellers are losing the capacity to drive the market lower. The next day, a strong white real body proves the bulls have taken over.
- **Signal & confirmation:** A signal that the bulls have seized control. The third line (strong white candle closing well into the first black body) is the confirmation candle.
- **Nison's notes:**
  - The pattern is named because, like the morning star (the planet Mercury) that foretells the sunrise, it presages higher prices.
  - The second gap (between the star and the third white candle) is rare, but its absence does not seem to vitiate the power of the formation.
  - Variations exist where there is more than one star in the middle; the third small real body session can itself be a hammer and a bullish engulfing line. If the third white confirmation candle is long enough, a black-candle/inverted-hammer/long-white combination can constitute a morning star pattern.

## Evening Star

- **Type:** Bearish · top reversal · three candle lines.
- **Structure:**
  - Line 1: Long, white real body (uptrend candle).
  - Line 2: A small real body (the star) that is the first hint of a top. Colour unimportant.
  - Line 3: A black real body that moves sharply into the first period's white real body.
- **Recognition:** In an uptrend, identify a long white real body, followed by a small real body (the star), followed by a black real body that closes substantially down into the first candle's white real body.
- **Psychology:** Traffic-light progression: the traffic light goes from green (the bullish white real body) to yellow (the star's warning signal) to red (the black real body confirms the prior trend has stopped).
- **Signal & confirmation:** The third black candle's intrusion into the first white candle's real body is the confirmation. The main concern should be the extent of the intrusion of the third day's black real body into the first day's white real body.
- **Nison's notes:**
  - The pattern is named because the evening star (the planet Venus) appears just before darkness sets in.
  - The pattern should be acted on if it arises after an uptrend.
  - In principle, an evening star should have a gap between the first and second real bodies and then another gap between the second and third real bodies. However, the second gap is rarely seen and is not necessary for the success of the pattern.
  - The evening star is similar to a Western island top reversal but furnishes a reversal signal not available with the island top. For an island top, the low of session 2 has to be above the highs of sessions 1 and 3. The evening star only requires the low of real body 2 to be above the high of real body 1 to be a reversal signal.
  - Evening stars can also be important at the top of a congestion band if they confirm another bearish signal, particularly when the star portion coincides with a resistance area.
  - Variations exist where the preceding white or following black real body is not long. Such variations can still be valid tops when reinforced by other evidence (e.g., a hanging-man line serving as the "star" portion, with the next day's opening under the hanging man's real body confirming a top).

## Evening Doji Star

- **Type:** Bearish · top reversal · three candle lines.
- **Structure:**
  - Line 1: Real body (typically white, uptrend candle).
  - Line 2: A doji (gapping up from the prior real body) — the star.
  - Line 3: A long, black real body that closes well into the first period's white real body.
- **Recognition:** Locate a doji whose real body gaps above the prior candle's real body during an uptrend, then identify a long black real body that closes well into the prior white real body.
- **Psychology:** The doji represents complete indecision after a powerful advance. When followed by a long black candle that closes deep into the prior white body, the prior uptrend's force is broken.
- **Signal & confirmation:** The session after the doji (a long, black real body closing well into the white real body) confirms a top reversal. The evening doji star is more important than the regular evening star because it contains a doji.
- **Nison's notes:**
  - It is a distinctive form of the regular evening star; the regular evening star has a small real body as its star, whereas the evening doji star has a doji as its star.
  - If the session after the doji star is a white candlestick which gaps higher, the bearish nature of the doji star is negated.
  - In equities, allow more flexibility with the gap requirement since stocks often open at, or very near, the prior session's close. A doji that does not gap above the prior white candlestick can still function as a doji star in equity markets.
  - Back-to-back evening doji star patterns can form on the chart and remain ominous.

## Morning Doji Star

- **Type:** Bullish · bottom reversal · three candle lines.
- **Structure:**
  - Line 1: A black real body (downtrend candle).
  - Line 2: A doji that gaps under the prior real body.
  - Line 3: A strong, white real body that closes well into the first period's black real body.
- **Recognition:** In a downtrend, identify a black real body, then a doji whose real body gaps below the prior black real body, then a strong white real body closing well into the prior black body.
- **Psychology:** The doji represents a complete stalemate after sustained selling. The strong white confirmation candle shows buyers overwhelming the prior downtrend.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Confirmation of a bottom reversal occurs if the next session is a strong white candlestick closing well into the black real body. This type of morning star can be a meaningful bottom.
- **Nison's notes:**
  - If, during a downtrend, a black candlestick gaps under the doji star, the potentially bullish implications of the doji star are voided.
  - It is important to wait for confirmation in the next session or two with doji stars.
  - A pattern is still called a star even when the shadow of the doji star overlaps the prior day's black real body. The doji star can still halt a prior price decline and be confirmed by a subsequent white real body.
  - The pattern can combine with other bullish evidence (such as a break under a support area that fails to hold) to strengthen the bottom signal.

## Abandoned Baby (Top and Bottom)

- **Type:** Major reversal · bearish (top) or bullish (bottom) · three candle lines.
- **Structure:**
  - **Abandoned Baby Top:** An upside gap doji star (the doji's shadows do not touch the prior candle) followed by a downside gap black candlestick whose shadows also do not touch the doji.
  - **Abandoned Baby Bottom:** A doji star with a gap before and after it (shadows do not touch) — the doji is isolated by gaps on both sides in a downtrend context.
- **Recognition:** Identify a doji whose shadows do not touch the prior candle and the following candle — i.e., the doji is isolated above (in an uptrend) or below (in a downtrend) by clean gaps.
- **Psychology:** The isolated doji represents a complete vacuum in pricing — neither bulls nor bears willing to transact near the prior levels — and the subsequent gap in the opposite direction confirms a wholesale rejection of the prior trend.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Considered a major top or bottom reversal signal. The abandoning of the doji by the gap in the opposite direction is itself the confirmation.
- **Nison's notes:** The abandoned baby is like a Western island top or bottom where the island session would be a doji. This pattern is very rare. The full effect requires that the shadows do not touch.

## Shooting Star

- **Type:** Bearish · top reversal warning · two-line pattern.
- **Structure:**
  - Line 1: Real body of any colour (the prior uptrend candle).
  - Line 2: A small real body at the lower end of its range with a long upper shadow. Colour of the real body is not important.
  - An ideal shooting star has a real body that gaps away from the prior real body.
- **Recognition:** Identify a candle with a small real body near the bottom of its daily range, with a long upper shadow extending well above the real body, occurring after an uptrend or at the top of a congestion zone.
- **Psychology:** The session opened near its low, then strongly rallied, and finally backed off to close near the opening. The session's rally could not be sustained. Pictorially, the market tells us the bulls attempted a push higher but failed.
- **Signal & confirmation:** A warning of an impending top. The shooting star is usually not a major reversal signal on its own (unlike the evening star). Confirmation should come from subsequent price action or resistance context.
- **Nison's notes:**
  - A shooting star shaped candlestick after a downturn is a bullish signal and is called an inverted hammer.
  - The gap is not always necessary. A shooting star without a gap from the prior real body can still be a significant reversal signal when viewed in context.
  - The pattern does not have to be perfectly formed to provide a valid signal. Always view candlestick lines and patterns in the context of the other technical evidence.
  - Variations include: (a) a shooting star with no gap between real bodies (still proves failure of bulls to maintain their drive); (b) a shooting star appearing in the middle of a trading range (should be viewed as a small real body / spinning top with little significance because indecision is expected in a range); (c) multiple shooting stars at a resistance level (each failed rally attempt at the same level reinforces the bearish signal).
  - Multiple failed rally attempts at the same resistance level raise the question of how long before the bulls give up; the eventual hammer or other reversal pattern often marks the end of the selloff that follows.

## Inverted Hammer

- **Type:** Bullish · bottom reversal · single candle line (requires confirmation).
- **Structure:** A candle with a small real body at the lower end of its range and a long upper shadow — visually identical to a shooting star line. The colour of the real body does not matter.
- **Recognition:** Identify a candle with a small real body near the bottom of its range, a long upper shadow, and a long lower shadow or position near the lows, occurring during a downtrend.
- **Psychology:** On the inverted hammer session the market opens on, or near, its low, then rallies. The bulls fail to sustain the rally and prices close at, or near, the lows of the session. Despite this bearish-appearing price action, if the next day opens above the real body of the inverted hammer, those who shorted at the opening or closing of the inverted hammer day are losing money. The longer the market holds above the inverted hammer's real body, the more likely these shorts will cover, sparking a short-covering rally. Bottom pickers going long could feed upon itself and a rally could be the result.
- **Signal & confirmation:** Bullish verification is required. Verification can take the form of the next day opening above the inverted hammer's real body (the larger the gap, the stronger the confirmation), or a white candlestick with higher prices. Other confirmatory patterns such as a bullish engulfing line in the following session can serve as confirmation.
- **Nison's notes:**
  - The inverted hammer is discussed alongside the shooting star because of its visual resemblance, but it is not a star pattern.
  - As with a regular hammer, the inverted hammer is a bullish pattern after a downtrend.
  - It is important to wait for bullish verification on the session following the inverted hammer.
  - The inverted hammer can become part of a morning star pattern if it is followed by a long white real body (the black real body before the inverted hammer, the inverted hammer, and the long white line after the hammer would make up a three-candlestick morning star pattern if the third white line is long enough).

## Three-River Historical Note

- The full name of the evening and morning star patterns are the **three-river evening star** and the **three-river morning star**.
- The origin comes from Nobunaga Oda, a major military figure of the late 16th century, who was one of the three military leaders who unified feudal Japan.
- He fought a seminal battle that occurred in a very fertile rice growing province. Since rice was a gauge of wealth, Nobunaga was as determined to wrest this area as the owners were to defend it.
- This fertile rice area had three rivers. The heavily defended area made it difficult for Nobunaga to cross these three rivers. Victory was his when his forces finally forded these three rivers.
- Hence the name "three-river" morning and evening star, where it is difficult to change the trend, yet victory for the attacking army is assured when the hurdle of the "three rivers" is crossed.

## Factors Increasing the Likelihood of a Star Reversal (Morning or Evening)

- A gap between the first candlestick's and the star's real bodies, AND a gap between the star's and the third candlestick's real bodies.
- The third candlestick closes deeply into the first candlestick's real body.
- Light volume on the first candlestick session and heavy volume on the third candlestick session. This shows a reduction of the force behind the prior trend and an increase in the direction force of the new trend.

## Support and Resistance Interaction with Star Patterns

- Old support often converts to new resistance — described as a very useful trading rule.
- The resistance level coinciding with the appearance of the evening star reinforces the negativeness of the pattern.
- Star patterns at the top of a congestion band can be important if they confirm another bearish signal.

## Flexibility, Context, and Market-Specific Rules

- Patterns do not have to be perfectly formed to provide a valid signal. Always view candlestick lines and patterns in the context of the other technical evidence.
- Certain candlestick configurations should have more latitude in the equity market because, unlike futures, stock prices may open relatively unchanged from the prior close. This means that specific patterns that relate the open to the prior day's close may have to be adjusted.
- A doji star's real body (that is, its opening and closing price) should be over the prior day's real body; in equity examples, allow flexibility when this strict rule is not met.
- Traders monitoring equity markets should experiment with candlestick techniques to discover which patterns may have to be modified.

## Star Pattern vs. Western Island Top

- The evening star furnishes a reversal signal not available with a Western island top.
- For an island top, the low of session 2 has to be above the highs of sessions 1 and 3.
- The evening star only requires the low of real body 2 to be above the high of real body 1 to be a reversal signal — a more relaxed condition.
- The abandoned baby is like a Western island top or bottom where the island session would be a doji.

## Glossary of Related Terms Used in the Excerpt

- **Star:** A small real body that gaps away from the large real body preceding it. The star's real body does not overlap the prior real body. Colour is not important.
- **Rain drop:** A star that occurs during a downtrend.
- **Doji star:** A doji that takes the place of the small real body in a star pattern.
- **Three-river morning/evening star:** Full historical name of the morning and evening star patterns.
- **Spinning top:** A small real body day reflecting indecision; in the middle of a trading range, indecision should be expected.
- **Hanging man:** A single-line pattern that can serve as the "star" portion of a variation evening star; the next day's opening under the hanging man's real body confirms a top.
- **Piercing pattern:** A bullish pattern referenced as a place where declines ended.
- **Bullish engulfing pattern:** A two-line bullish reversal pattern cited as confirmatory price action following inverted hammers and morning doji stars.
